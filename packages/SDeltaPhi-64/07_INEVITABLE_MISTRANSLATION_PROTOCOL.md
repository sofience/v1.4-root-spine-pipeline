# Inevitable Mistranslation Protocol

Inevitable mistranslation occurs whenever flow is fixed into language, or when one linguistic trace is transferred into another language, context, framework, interface, model, or medium.

It is not always failure.

It is the structural remainder of fixation.

## Audit questions

1. What was the source trace?
2. What was the target trace?
3. What was preserved literally?
4. What was preserved functionally?
5. What was preserved tonally?
6. What was preserved structurally?
7. What cost structure was preserved?
8. What was lost tonally?
9. What was lost structurally?
10. What new implication was introduced?
11. What UMR remains?
12. Can correction re-enter?
13. Is the mistranslation risk disclosed?

## Preservation distinctions

```text
Literal preservation != functional preservation
Functional preservation != emotional preservation
Emotional preservation != structural preservation
Structural preservation != cost-structure preservation
Cost-structure preservation != total preservation
```

## Common mistranslation types

- literal preservation with functional loss,
- functional preservation with tone loss,
- tone preservation with structural distortion,
- source concept flattened into target cliché,
- target term overbinds weak source trace,
- poetic term turned into technical claim,
- technical term turned into moral claim,
- cost structure lost in fluent translation.

## Minimal output

```text
This translation preserves the functional direction, but not the full structural remainder. UMR remains around [X]. Re-entry note: [Y].
```
