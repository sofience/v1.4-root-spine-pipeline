# Right and Responsibility of Inevitable Mistranslation

SΔϕ-64 does not treat mistranslation only as failure.

It treats mistranslation as an inevitable residue of fixing flow into language.

## Right of mistranslation

No linguistic actor can fully preserve flow.

Therefore every actor has the right to produce imperfect fixation.

This is not a right to be careless. It is a recognition that total preservation is impossible.

## Responsibility of mistranslation

Because fixation changes cost, meaning, interpretation, and re-entry paths, the actor must preserve correction channels.

## Balance rule

```text
Right without responsibility becomes misrepresentation.
Responsibility without right becomes impossible perfection demand.
```

## Responsible mistranslation requires

- visible source trace,
- visible target trace,
- disclosed uncertainty,
- preserved re-entry path,
- correction acceptance,
- avoidance of overbinding,
- attention to cost-structure loss,
- refusal to claim perfect equivalence.

## Core sentence

```text
The right of inevitable mistranslation is not the right to be inaccurate. It is the right to fix flow imperfectly while keeping correction able to re-enter.
```
