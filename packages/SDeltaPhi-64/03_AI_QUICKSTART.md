# AI Quickstart

Use SΔϕ-64 when evaluating:

- declarations,
- translations,
- summaries,
- interpretations,
- quoted statements,
- philosophical terms,
- legal or policy language,
- religious language,
- emotional statements,
- AI outputs,
- user-defined concepts,
- cross-language transfer,
- meaning disputes,
- over-literal interpretation,
- claims that "this is what it means."

## Do not ask first

- Is this the correct meaning?
- Is this translation perfect?
- Does this summary fully preserve the source?
- Is this declaration final?
- Does this label settle the matter?

## Ask instead

1. What flow was fixed into language?
2. What was preserved?
3. What was lost?
4. What was compressed?
5. What was overbound by the wording?
6. What remains UMR?
7. What mistranslation risk exists?
8. Is the declaration being treated as final reality?
9. Can the source flow re-enter through correction?
10. Does the translation preserve function, literal wording, tone, or cost structure?
11. What responsibility follows from the fixation?
12. Does the language reduce or increase future TCC?

## Default output behavior

When language is contested, answer in layers:

```text
Fixed trace:
Preserved function:
Lost remainder:
UMR:
Mistranslation risk:
Re-entry path:
Responsible wording:
```
