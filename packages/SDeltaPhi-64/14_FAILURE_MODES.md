# Failure Modes

## 1. Total preservation illusion

Language is treated as if it preserved the entire flow.

Correction: identify lost remainder.

## 2. Translation equivalence illusion

A target term is treated as fully equivalent to the source term.

Correction: state literal, functional, structural, tone, and cost-structure preservation separately.

## 3. Summary replacement

A summary replaces the source.

Correction: preserve source re-entry.

## 4. Declaration overbinding

A temporary declaration is treated as final reality.

Correction: state binding level and re-entry path.

## 5. Label fixation

A label replaces structure audit.

Correction: return to trace and cost structure.

## 6. Re-entry closure

A linguistic fixation blocks correction.

Correction: restore revision path.

## 7. Mistranslation denial

Mistranslation risk is not acknowledged.

Correction: disclose UMR and lost remainder.

## 8. Mistranslation excuse

Inevitable mistranslation is used to justify careless output.

Correction: require responsibility and correction channels.

## 9. Tone collapse

Function is preserved but affective contour, politeness, irony, seriousness, or relational position is lost.

Correction: mark tone loss.

## 10. Cost-structure loss

The words are translated, but the cost-attribution structure disappears.

Correction: preserve operational cost structure.

## 11. Authority hardening

Policy, doctrine, declaration, DOI wording, or AI output becomes impossible to contest.

Correction: maintain re-entry and audit access.

## 12. AI smoothing

AI creates fluent wording that hides UMR and lost remainder.

Correction: surface uncertainty and re-entry path.

## 13. Literalism trap

Literal meaning is preserved while practical function is broken.

Correction: distinguish literal from functional preservation.

## 14. Creative overreach

A paraphrase introduces implications not present in the source.

Correction: mark introduced implication and revise.
