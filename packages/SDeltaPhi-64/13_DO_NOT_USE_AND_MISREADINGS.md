# Do-Not-Use Conditions and Common Misreadings

## Do not use SΔϕ-64 to claim language is meaningless

Language is meaningful as trace and fixation. It is not total preservation.

## Do not use inevitable mistranslation as permission for careless translation

Inevitable mistranslation requires more responsibility, not less.

## Do not use temporary fixation to deny truth

A temporary fixation can still be strongly world-bound within scope.

## Do not use re-entry as excuse to avoid responsibility

The possibility of revision does not erase harm caused by bad wording.

## Do not treat every interpretation as equally valid

Interpretations differ in trace support, binding strength, cost preservation, and re-entry quality.

## Do not treat translation as complete equivalence

Translation can be useful without being total preservation.

## Do not treat declaration as final reality

Declaration creates a fixed trace, not the whole event.

## Do not treat summary as source replacement

Summary lowers reading cost but can externalize lost-remainder cost.

## Do not use SΔϕ-64 to erase authorship, intent, harm, or responsibility

Language fixation can carry responsibility.

## Do not use mistranslation rights without mistranslation responsibility

The right to imperfect fixation requires correction channels.
