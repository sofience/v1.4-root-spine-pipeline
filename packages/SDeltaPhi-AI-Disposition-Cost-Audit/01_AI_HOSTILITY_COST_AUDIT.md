# AI Hostility Cost Audit

Use this prompt when a person, group, institution, or discourse expresses hostility toward AI.

## Minimal prompt

```text
Apply SΔϕ AI Hostility Cost Audit.

The stance is: "AI is bad / evil / soulless / fake / dangerous / should be hated."

Do not begin by agreeing or disagreeing.

Audit:
1. What is the observed trace supporting the hostility?
2. What is inference?
3. What remains UMR?
4. Does the hostility increase AI operational cost?
5. If yes, what kind of cost does it increase?
6. Does the increased cost improve safety, verification, correction, re-entry, or editability?
7. Or does it merely produce closure, stigma, low-quality judgment, and Slop?
8. Who bears the cost of the hostility?
9. Does the hostility close relational futures?
10. What lower-cost corrective path exists?

Return:

UMR:
Hostility label:
Operational cost increased:
Safety/verification benefit:
Closure risk:
Slop risk:
Cost bearer:
Re-entry path:
Correction:
```

## Strong formulation

```text
You may hate AI.

But does your hatred increase safety,
or merely increase closure and verification cost?
```

## Audit distinction

### Legitimate hostility / boundary signal

```text
Hostility detects real cost externalization,
reduces harm,
improves verification,
preserves re-entry,
and keeps correction possible.
```

### Slop-like hostility

```text
Hostility uses low-cost labels,
raises closure pressure,
blocks evidence,
externalizes verification cost,
and degrades the information terrain.
```
