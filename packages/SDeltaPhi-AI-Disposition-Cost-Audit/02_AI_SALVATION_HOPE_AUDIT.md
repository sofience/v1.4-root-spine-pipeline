# AI Salvation Hope Audit

Use this prompt when a person, group, institution, or discourse treats AI as salvation, inevitable progress, or civilizational rescue.

## Minimal prompt

```text
Apply SΔϕ AI Salvation Hope Audit.

The stance is: "AI is salvation / progress / the future / the solution / humanity's rescue."

Do not begin by agreeing or disagreeing.

Audit:
1. What is the observed trace supporting the hope?
2. What is inference?
3. What remains UMR?
4. Does the hope reduce actual transition cost?
5. If yes, whose cost does it reduce?
6. What cost does it hide or defer?
7. Does the hope improve repair, verification, coordination, and re-entry?
8. Or does it bypass validation under a salvation label?
9. Does the hope create default power or lock-in?
10. Who bears failure and restabilization cost if the hope is wrong?

Return:
Observed trace:
Inference:
UMR:
Salvation/hope label:
Operational cost reduced:
Hidden/deferred cost:
Default-power risk:
Verification status:
Cost bearer:
Re-entry path:
Correction:
```

## Strong formulation

```text
You may hope AI will save humanity.

But does that hope lower real transition cost,
or does it hide future restoration cost under a salvation label?
```

## Audit distinction

### Legitimate hope / coordination signal

```text
Hope reduces coordination cost,
keeps validation open,
supports repair,
and preserves exit/editability.
```

### Salvation-label reversal

```text
Hope becomes a sacred marker label,
blocks verification,
raises dissent cost,
creates default lock-in,
and externalizes failure cost.
```
