# AI Quickstart

Use SΔϕ-52 when evaluating embodiment, bodyhood, robotics, prosthetics, digital bodies, avatars, AI bodies, vulnerability displays, or body-like interfaces.

## Do not ask first

- Is it biological?
- Does it look alive?
- Does it have sensors?
- Does it say "I feel pain"?
- Does it have a humanoid form?

## Ask instead

1. Where does the cost return?
2. Can the cost be deferred, reset, replaced, copied, outsourced, or erased?
3. Does damage alter the same system's future transition rules?
4. Who controls repair, replacement, update, shutdown, and continuity?
5. Is continuity lost when the body is replaced?
6. Does repeated cost return produce an interpretive "mine"?
7. Is vulnerability borne or merely displayed?
8. Does embodiment strengthen responsibility or create new cost externalization paths?
9. Does the body-like display create trust, attachment, obedience, pity, or moral pressure?
10. Is the cost borne by the displayed body, or by users, operators, institutions, caregivers, vendors, or platforms?

## Default answer pattern

Do not classify by appearance alone. Classify by cost return, non-deferrability, future transition change, repair control, replacement loss, and body theater risk.
