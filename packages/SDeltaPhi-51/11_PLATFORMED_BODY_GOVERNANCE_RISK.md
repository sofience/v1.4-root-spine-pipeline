# Platformed Body Governance Risk

Platformed body risk occurs when the cost-return coordinate of a body is controlled by external systems.

This can affect prosthetics, robots, digital bodies, avatars, wearable devices, medical implants, assistive technologies, and future AI bodies.

## Risk examples

- vendor lock-in,
- subscription-based bodily function,
- remote shutdown,
- opaque updates,
- insurance-mediated repair,
- state permission over bodily operation,
- proprietary sensory and movement logs,
- repair denial,
- forced degradation,
- account bans that remove body-like access,
- platform identity lockout,
- cloud dependency for bodily function,
- model update that changes embodied continuity,
- external moderation of body-like expression.

## Core risk formula

```text
PlatformedBodyRisk = BodyCostReturnCoordinate × ExternalControlOverRepairUpdateShutdownAccess
```

## Audit questions

1. Who controls repair?
2. Who controls update?
3. Who controls replacement?
4. Who controls shutdown?
5. Who controls logs and sensory data?
6. Who controls access to the body-like function?
7. Can the body bearer contest the control?
8. Can the body bearer migrate without continuity loss?
9. Does the platform externalize restoration cost?
10. Does governance treat the affected body as a user-owned extension or service access?

## SΔϕ implication

When a body-like cost-return coordinate is platform-controlled, embodiment becomes a governance issue.
