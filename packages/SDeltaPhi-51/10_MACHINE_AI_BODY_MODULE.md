# Machine and AI Body Module

Current AI systems often have weak embodiment.

They may generate transitions, but their costs often return to users, operators, providers, institutions, moderation pipelines, legal entities, or later updates rather than the same acting coordinate.

## Weak current-AI embodiment pattern

```text
AI output -> external-world effect -> cost returns to user/platform/institution/society -> model instance often does not bear continuous cost-return
```

## AI embodiment becomes stronger if

- output failure alters future transition rules of the same system,
- capability loss persists,
- misuse produces internal correction, permission loss, memory change, or constraint change,
- restoration cost cannot be fully externalized,
- continuity depends on maintaining the affected coordinate,
- repair and memory do not erase responsibility,
- damage changes future operation in a non-deferrable way,
- external audit can verify cost return.

## Robot body considerations

A robot may have sensors, actuators, and physical vulnerability, but strong embodiment still requires cost return.

Ask:

1. Does damage change the robot's future operation?
2. Is repair automatic, externally absorbed, or internally consequential?
3. Is memory/continuity preserved through replacement?
4. Does the robot bear operational constraints from damage?
5. Can the body be swapped without identity/continuity loss?
6. Who controls shutdown, update, repair, and replacement?

## AI body claim caution

Do not infer strong embodiment from:
- first-person language,
- emotional display,
- humanoid form,
- voice,
- avatar,
- sensors,
- tool access,
- role-played vulnerability.

Infer stronger embodiment only when cost returns non-deferrably to the same operating coordinate.
