# Prosthetic and Extended Body Module

A prosthetic begins as an external tool.

It becomes more bodily as failure, repair, skill, dependence, performance, and restoration cost return to the user's future path.

## Progression

```text
External tool -> embodied tool -> extended body -> strong extension
```

## Audit questions

1. Is the device merely used, or integrated into skill and expectation?
2. Does its failure change the user's action space?
3. Does repair cost return to the user?
4. Is the user's self-image, perception, action, or dependence altered?
5. Who controls maintenance, update, replacement, and access?
6. Can the user continue without it, and at what cost?
7. Does replacement create continuity loss?
8. Does the device become part of the user's "I can" field?
9. Does external control over the device become control over the user's body-like capacity?

## Platformed prosthetic risk

A prosthetic or assistive technology may become part of the extended body while remaining controlled by vendors, insurers, states, platforms, licensing systems, or opaque update pipelines.

This creates a governance problem:

```text
External control over repair/update/access -> external control over body-cost coordinate
```

## Do not use

Do not reduce prosthetics to tools merely because they are manufactured.

Do not automatically treat every device as a body. Inspect cost return and continuity.
