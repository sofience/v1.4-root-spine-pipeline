# Do-Not-Use Conditions and Common Misreadings

## Do not use SΔϕ-52 to deny the importance of biological bodies

Biological bodies are often strong examples of non-deferrable cost return. SΔϕ-52 does not erase biology. It prevents bodyhood from being reduced to biology alone.

## Do not use SΔϕ-52 to grant embodiment by appearance

A robot, avatar, AI interface, or fictional character is not strongly embodied merely because it looks alive, speaks in first person, or displays vulnerability.

## Do not use SΔϕ-52 to prove AI suffering or AI subjecthood

Body-cost return can be relevant to suffering and subjecthood debates, but it does not prove them by itself.

## Do not use SΔϕ-52 to deny possible non-biological embodiment

If cost returns non-deferrably to a non-biological system and changes future transition conditions, the embodiment claim should remain open to audit.

## Do not use SΔϕ-52 as medical, legal, disability, prosthetic, or clinical advice

This is an operational ontology and audit grammar, not professional advice.

## Do not use "body theater" as a moral insult

Body theater is a classification for body-like signs without corresponding non-deferrable cost return.

## Do not treat replaceability alone as proof of no cost

Ask whether replacement itself creates continuity cost, memory loss, identity disruption, dependency shock, or governance risk.

## Do not ignore external control

A body-like system whose repair, update, shutdown, or access is externally controlled may create platformed body governance risk.
