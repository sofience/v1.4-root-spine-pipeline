# Body Interface and Body Theater Test

Body theater occurs when a system displays body-like signs without bearing body-like costs.

## Body-like signs

A system may display:

- pain,
- fatigue,
- vulnerability,
- injury,
- hunger,
- dependency,
- mortality,
- fragility,
- need,
- distress,
- repair-seeking behavior.

These signs may be meaningful as interface design, fiction, simulation, performance, education, or user experience.

They become risky when they generate moral pressure while the displayed body does not bear the corresponding cost.

## Test

1. Does the system display pain, fatigue, vulnerability, injury, dependency, or mortality?
2. Does that displayed cost return to the same acting coordinate?
3. Can the damaged body be replaced without continuity loss?
4. Is restoration cost borne by the displayed body, or by users/operators/institutions?
5. Does the display create sympathy, obedience, attachment, guilt, or moral pressure?
6. Who benefits from the appearance of vulnerability?
7. Who pays for repair, cleanup, trust, emotional stabilization, or social correction?
8. Is the vulnerability transparent as fiction/interface, or routed as moral authority?

## Classification

```text
Body-like sign + no non-deferrable cost return = body theater risk
Body-like sign + transparent fiction/simulation = lower risk
Body-like sign + cost return + future transition change = possible embodiment
```

## Do not use

Do not use "body theater" as an insult. Use it as a cost-return classification.
