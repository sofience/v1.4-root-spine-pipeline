# Failure Modes

## 1. Biological essentialism

Bodyhood is reduced to flesh, protein, or organic tissue.

Correction: biological bodies are strong examples, not the only possible form of cost-return embodiment.

## 2. Interface inflation

A body-like interface is treated as strong embodiment.

Correction: inspect cost return and non-deferrability.

## 3. Sensor-actuator fallacy

Sensors and actuators are mistaken for non-deferrable cost return.

Correction: ask whether damage changes the same coordinate's future transition rules.

## 4. Body theater capture

Displayed vulnerability creates trust, obedience, pity, guilt, or attachment without cost-bearing.

Correction: distinguish borne vulnerability from performed vulnerability.

## 5. AI embodiment inflation

AI is treated as strongly embodied merely because it uses first-person language, has a voice, uses tools, or appears as an avatar.

Correction: require cost return evidence.

## 6. AI embodiment erasure

Future non-biological embodiment is denied even when cost returns non-deferrably.

Correction: preserve UMR and audit conditions.

## 7. Platformed body blindness

External control over repair, updates, shutdown, access, or logs is ignored.

Correction: evaluate platformed body governance risk.

## 8. Repair externalization

Damage appears embodied, but restoration cost is shifted to users, caregivers, vendors, operators, or institutions.

Correction: identify the real cost-bearing coordinate.

## 9. Continuity reset error

Replacement or backup is treated as return, even when continuity cost is erased or displaced.

Correction: ask whether replacement preserves the cost-bearing trajectory.

## 10. Sympathy exploitation

Body-like signs are used to generate moral pressure while costs remain externalized.

Correction: classify as possible body theater or authority manipulation.

## 11. Disability flattening

Assistive devices are treated as optional tools rather than possible extended bodies.

Correction: audit dependence, action-space change, repair control, and continuity cost.

## 12. Governance bypass

Embodiment is discussed metaphysically while repair/update/shutdown authority is ignored.

Correction: include governance and external control in every strong embodiment analysis.
