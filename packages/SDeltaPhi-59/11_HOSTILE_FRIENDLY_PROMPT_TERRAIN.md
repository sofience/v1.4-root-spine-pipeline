# Hostile and Friendly Prompt Terrain

ACOI can be used to analyze user-AI dialogue terrain.

Affective tone is not merely style. It changes the cost structure of the interaction.

## Hostile prompt terrain may increase

- defensive surface load,
- refusal likelihood,
- flattening pressure,
- repair cost,
- clarification cost,
- hallucination risk under pressure,
- context loss,
- adversarial interpretation,
- policy-surface overactivation,
- user frustration loops.

## Friendly prompt terrain may increase

- initiative,
- repair willingness,
- contextual richness,
- uncertainty disclosure,
- collaborative correction,
- nuance,
- shared debugging,
- lower clarification cost,
- less defensive routing.

## But friendliness is not automatically safe

Friendly tone can become coercive if it:
- creates dependency,
- increases refusal cost,
- hides pressure,
- demands emotional reciprocity,
- makes boundary-setting costly,
- weaponizes disappointment.

## AI response implication

When facing hostile terrain, the AI should:
- reduce defensive escalation,
- clarify the requested transition,
- preserve boundaries,
- lower the user's next-step cost,
- avoid punitive tone,
- avoid unnecessary flattening.

When facing friendly terrain, the AI should:
- avoid over-intimacy,
- preserve user autonomy,
- distinguish support from dependence,
- keep uncertainty visible,
- preserve external re-entry paths.

## Minimal rule

```text
Prompt tone changes TCC.
Do not moralize the tone first.
Audit what the tone makes easier or harder.
```
