# Care, Dependency, and Coercion Test

Use this module when care, love, protection, concern, safety, loyalty, or support may also function as pressure.

## Care test

1. Does the act reduce the other's burden?
2. Does it preserve the other's refusal?
3. Does it open repair?
4. Does it share stabilization cost?
5. Does it reduce recurrence cost?
6. Does it remain editable when challenged?

## Dependency test

1. Does the support increase future exit cost?
2. Does the other become less able to act without the supporter?
3. Does help become a gatekeeping mechanism?
4. Does emotional relief require continued compliance?
5. Does the actor benefit from the other's dependence?
6. Is gratitude demanded as repayment?

## Coercion-laundering test

1. Is pressure disguised as care, love, concern, safety, protection, or benefit?
2. Does refusal become emotionally expensive?
3. Is vulnerability used to control future action?
4. Does the actor say "for your own good" while closing alternatives?
5. Does the relationship punish boundary-setting?
6. Does the affective claim block re-entry, correction, or external audit?

## Output classification

```text
Supportive care:
reduces cost + preserves refusal + opens repair

Ambiguous care:
reduces some cost but increases dependency or refusal cost

Coercive care:
uses care language to raise refusal cost, transfer burden, or close alternatives

Care theater:
signals care without cost sharing, repair, or recurrence reduction
```

## Minimal answer

```text
The question is not whether it sounds caring. The question is whether it lowers the other's cost while preserving refusal and repair.
```
