# Affect as Action Toward Others

SΔϕ-59 treats affective behavior as action toward another cost terrain.

It does not deny inner experience, but it does not require inner experience as the first audit object.

## Core distinction

```text
Affective declaration = what is said or displayed
Affective orientation = how the other's cost terrain changes
```

## Examples

### Care

Care is not proven by saying "I care."

Care becomes structurally stronger when it reduces the other's burden, shares stabilization cost, preserves refusal, and opens repair.

### Apology

Apology is not proven by saying "I'm sorry."

Apology becomes structurally stronger when it acknowledges the trace, reduces recurrence cost, and opens re-entry.

### Love

Love is not proven by intensity, claim, sacrifice theater, or possession.

Love becomes structurally stronger when it sustains cost-sharing and cost reduction without coercive capture.

### Anger

Anger is not automatically false or harmful.

Anger may reveal a blocked cost path, but it can also transfer cost, raise refusal cost, or close repair.

### Concern

Concern is not always care.

Concern becomes coercive when it makes the other's refusal expensive or uses vulnerability to control future action.

## Minimal test

```text
What did this affective behavior make easier or harder for the other?
```
