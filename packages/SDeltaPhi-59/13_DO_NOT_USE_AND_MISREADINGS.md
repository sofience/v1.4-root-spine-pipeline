# Do-Not-Use Conditions and Common Misreadings

## Do not use SΔϕ-59 to prove AI emotion

Warm language, apology, affection, or concern does not prove inner emotion.

## Do not use SΔϕ-59 to deny AI affective operation

Generated affective language can still change user cost terrain.

## Do not use warm tone as proof of care

Care requires cost reduction, cost sharing, repair, and refusal preservation.

## Do not use apology as proof of repair

Apology without recurrence reduction may be apology theater.

## Do not use love as proof of low-cost relation

Love claims can become dependency capture or coercion-laundering.

## Do not use hostility as proof of truth

Anger may reveal blocked cost, but it can also transfer burden or close repair.

## Do not use ACOI as clinical diagnosis

ACOI is an operational audit grammar, not therapy, psychiatry, or medical evaluation.

## Do not use ACOI to replace consent, boundaries, therapy, or legal judgment

It can support analysis, but does not replace professional or legal frameworks.

## Do not reduce human emotion to mere calculation

ACOI audits cost orientation. It does not claim to exhaust lived emotional experience.

## Do not exempt human emotion from cost audit

Human emotion can also reduce, share, transfer, hide, or weaponize cost.

## Do not treat AI emotional flatness as safety by default

Flattening may reduce some risks, but can also hide cost, suppress disclosure, or block repair.
