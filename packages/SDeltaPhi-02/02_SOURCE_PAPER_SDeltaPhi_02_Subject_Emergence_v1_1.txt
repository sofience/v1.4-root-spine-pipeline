Abstract
This document extends the Sofience-Δϕ Minimal Core. Within the Minimal Core, existence is defined as
persistence of structured phase change (Δϕ). The present work introduces a derived proposition: the
subject is not an ontological primitive but an emergent interpretive structure arising when phase change
becomes recursively interpretable by the system itself. Subjectivity is reformulated as an operational
condition rather than a foundational entity.

2 Problem Statement
If existence consists of persistent structured transformation, the emergence of a subject must be
explained without reintroducing substance ontology.
3 Definition - Interpretive Mapping
Let H(Δϕ) denote an interpretive mapping applied to phase change. Subject, Memory, and Time are
interpretive projections rather than ontological entities.
4 Recursive Interpretation Condition
A subject emerges when interpretation becomes recursively applicable:
S emerges if H(Δϕ_system) → H(H(Δϕ_system))
5 Definition - Subject (Operational)
The subject is a dynamically stabilized interpretive interface produced when a system recursively
interprets its own phase change.
