Abstract
This note binds three minimal operational notions—Agency, Responsibility, and
Freedom—into a conditional closure. Rather than asserting necessity by definition, it
specifies minimal assumptions under which the ARF triangle becomes the smallest
stable description of recursive transition systems under irreversibility and stabilization
pressure. In this binding, Freedom is treated as an emergent operational term: it
appears when agency-updates and responsibility-constraints co-act, and the resulting
path-differences are kept alive by minimal interpretation (Hmin).
0. Position in the Series
SΔϕ-08 is a binding note over prior minimal axioms. It does not introduce a new
primitive; it states when and why the primitives close into a stable triangle.
Referenced nodes: SΔϕ-05 (Agency), SΔϕ-06 (Responsibility), SΔϕ-07 (Hmin / minimal
interpretation).
1. Minimal Assumptions (A0–A2)
1
A0 (Irreversibility). There exist transitions whose effects cannot be perfectly
undone.
2
A1 (Stabilization pressure). Repeated transitions generate drift or self-damage risk
unless constrained; such risk can be treated as an effective cost.
3
A2 (Difference re-entry via minimal interpretation). A minimal operator Hmin exists
that (i) marks differences between transition-paths and (ii) re-enters those marks
into subsequent state-generation.
2. Minimal Definitions (D1–D3)
Let the system state be φ(t), and the transition generator be Gt such that:
φ(t+1) = Gt(φ(t), ·)
D1. Agency (from SΔϕ-05). Agency is present when the system takes its own
transition law as an operational object and updates it in a way that persistently affects
future state generation:
Gt+1 = Update(Gt; Δφ(t))
D2. Responsibility (from SΔϕ-06). Responsibility is present when irreversible costs
produced by transitions become internal constraints that shape subsequent
transitions:
Ct+1 = Internalize(Cost(Δφ(t))) ; Gt+1 is constrained by Ct+1
D3. Freedom (operational; tied to SΔϕ-07). Freedom is not “number of options,” but
persistent re-entry of transition-differences:
F(t) = ReEntry(Hmin(Δφ))

3. Freedom as the Third Vertex
Agency updates transition laws; Responsibility internalizes irreversible costs.
However, Agency + Responsibility alone tends to push the system toward
single-attractor convergence (lock-in): stability is achieved by collapsing viable
variation into one dominant basin.
In this series, the third vertex is not chosen as an extra metaphysical ingredient.
Instead, it is the minimal operational residue that must survive if the coupled system
is to remain open: when (i) laws can be updated (Agency) and (ii) costs are
internalized (Responsibility), the system still needs a mechanism by which
path-differences persist and re-enter subsequent state-generation. That mechanism is
denoted as Freedom and is supplied by Hmin (difference marking + re-entry).
Equivalently: Freedom is emergent between Agency and Responsibility insofar as their
coupling produces differences that can either be erased by stabilization (lock-in) or
conserved as re-entering marks (operational openness).
F(t) = ReEntry(Hmin(Δφ(t))) (emergent term of the Agency–Responsibility coupling)
4. Conditional Closure Propositions (C1′–C3′)
1
C1′ (Agency ⇒ Responsibility, under A0–A1). Under irreversibility and stabilization
pressure, sustained transition-law updates generate cumulative drift or damage
risk. If such risk is not internalized as constraint, agency-driven updates drift
without bound. Thus, to keep agency stable over time, the system must internalize
irreversible costs as constraints.
2
C2′ (Responsibility ⇒ stabilized Agency, under A0–A1). When irreversible costs are
internalized, updates to the transition law are regularized by constraints. This turns
law-updating from uncontrolled drift into persistent, bounded modification.
3
C3′ (Freedom ⇔ persistent difference re-entry, under A2). Under A2, freedom is
equivalent to continued re-entry of marked differences into transition generation.
When Hmin collapses (difference marking or re-entry fails), multiple physical
transitions may remain but become operationally indistinguishable.
5. Collapse Modes (diagnostic triangle)
These are operational failure modes, not metaphysical claims:
1
Agency without Responsibility → Drift. Law updates occur without cost constraints;
update dynamics are unbounded.
2
Responsibility without Freedom → Lock-in. Costs are internalized but differences do
not re-enter; the system converges into a single attractor.

6. Minimal ARF Recurrence (compact form)
A compact operational coupling can be written as:
F(t) = ReEntry(Hmin(Δφ(t)))
C(t+1) = Internalize(Cost(Δφ(t)))
Gt+1 = Update(Gt; C(t+1), F(t))
Freedom supplies re-entered differences; Responsibility supplies internalized costs;
Agency updates the law under both.
7. Scope note
ARF closure is asserted only under assumptions A0–A2. Outside these assumptions,
ARF may not close, or alternative vertices may be required.
