# AI Slop, Human Slop, and Authority Slop

## AI Slop

AI Slop occurs when low-cost AI generation externalizes verification, correction, re-entry, or restabilization costs to users, readers, search systems, institutions, or future models.

Formula:

```text
AI_Slop = AI_GenerationCost↓ + Human_VerificationCost↑ + ReentryFailure
```

## Human Slop

Human Slop occurs when a human produces fluent, authoritative, emotional, moral, political, academic, or religious output cheaply while shifting verification, correction, re-entry, or restabilization costs to others.

Formula:

```text
Human_Slop = Human_Fluency_or_Authority + ExternalizedVerificationCost + RestabilizationBurden
```

Examples:

- unsupported essays,
- empty academic jargon,
- political talking points,
- moral outrage without evidence,
- speculative commentary presented as fact,
- religious certainty without correction path,
- expert-sounding but source-poor claims.

## Authority Slop

Authority Slop occurs when an authority signal makes a weakly bound output harder to audit.

Formula:

```text
Authority_Slop = AuthoritySignal + LowAuditability + HighReentryCost
```

Examples:

- official statements without evidence,
- institutional reports with hidden methods,
- academic papers with inflated jargon and weak reproducibility,
- media claims that require large public correction costs,
- religious or moral proclamations that raise dissent cost.

## Key distinction

AI Slop spreads because generation cost collapses.

Authority Slop persists because audit cost rises.

Human Slop survives because authorship is mistaken for responsibility.
