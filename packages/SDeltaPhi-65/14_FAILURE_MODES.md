# Failure Modes

## 1. AI-source overbinding

AI-generated output is automatically classified as Slop.

Correction: audit verification, correction, re-entry, and restabilization cost.

## 2. Human-authorship exemption

Human-authored output is exempted from Slop analysis.

Correction: inspect cost externalization regardless of source.

## 3. Aesthetic reduction

Slop is treated as ugly, boring, generic, or low taste.

Correction: focus on externalized restabilization cost.

## 4. Authority exemption

Expert, institutional, sacred, or academic output is assumed non-Slop.

Correction: inspect authority pollution and re-entry cost.

## 5. Slop stigma weaponization

Slop is used to dismiss authors, groups, or low-status speakers without audit.

Correction: require trace and cost analysis.

## 6. Re-entry omission

The output is judged without asking whether the reader can return to source, evidence, method, or revision.

Correction: perform re-entry audit.

## 7. Verification burden blindness

The analysis ignores who must verify the output.

Correction: identify verification burden carrier.

## 8. Restabilization invisibility

The analysis ignores who restores polluted information terrain.

Correction: identify restabilization burden.

## 9. Fiction confusion

Clearly fictional or creative output is treated as Slop because it is generated.

Correction: check whether verification is expected.

## 10. Human Slop denial

Political, academic, religious, corporate, or journalistic Slop is ignored because it is human-made.

Correction: apply the same cost criterion.

## 11. Over-policing

The Slop label becomes a tool for suppressing output rather than auditing cost.

Correction: preserve UMR and revision path.

## 12. Source-only classification

The output is classified only by creator type.

Correction: source type is metadata, not Slop criterion.
