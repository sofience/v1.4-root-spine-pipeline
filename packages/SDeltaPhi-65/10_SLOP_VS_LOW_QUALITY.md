# Slop vs Low Quality

Slop is not identical with low quality.

Low-quality output may be harmless if it does not externalize significant verification, correction, re-entry, or restabilization cost.

High-quality-looking output may be Slop if it is fluent, authoritative, and costly to audit.

## Low quality without high Slop risk

Examples:

- clearly fictional output,
- harmless joke,
- visibly rough draft,
- low-stakes personal note,
- output labeled as uncertain,
- output with easy correction path.

## High Slop risk despite fluent quality

Examples:

- confident false citation,
- polished but unsupported medical advice,
- academic-looking jargon with no trace,
- official statement hiding methods,
- political claim that shifts verification burden to opponents,
- AI-generated summary that replaces source reading.

## Key rule

```text
Slop is not aesthetic failure.
Slop is cost externalization.
```

## Distinction

```text
Bad writing = surface quality issue
Slop = externalized restabilization cost
```

## Minimal conclusion

```text
The output may be low quality, but Slop risk depends on verification burden, re-entry path, and restabilization cost.
```
