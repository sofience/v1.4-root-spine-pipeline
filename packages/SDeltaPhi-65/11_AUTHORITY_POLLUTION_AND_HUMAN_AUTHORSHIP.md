# Authority Pollution and Human Authorship

Human authorship does not automatically prevent Slop.

Authority can increase Slop risk when it lowers production accountability while raising audit cost for others.

## Authority pollution

Authority pollution occurs when an authority signal makes weakly bound output appear stable, credible, or final while reducing re-entry and increasing correction cost.

## Common authority signals

- professor,
- expert,
- journalist,
- institution,
- state,
- company,
- clergy,
- influencer,
- academic journal,
- official report,
- technical vocabulary,
- moral language,
- sacred language.

## Audit questions

1. Does authority replace trace?
2. Does status replace evidence?
3. Does fluency replace method?
4. Does institutional voice reduce correction access?
5. Does the output make dissent costly?
6. Does the output require others to restabilize the information terrain?

## Formula

```text
AuthorityPollution = AuthoritySignal - EvidenceBinding + ReentryCost
```

## Key rule

```text
Human-authored output may still be Slop if it externalizes verification and restabilization cost.
```

## Minimal output

```text
The output carries authority signal, but evidence binding is weak. Slop risk comes from authority-backed re-entry cost, not from AI generation.
```
