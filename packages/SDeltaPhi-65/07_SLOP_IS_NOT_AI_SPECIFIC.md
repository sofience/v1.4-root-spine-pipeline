# Slop Is Not AI-Specific

## Core claim

Slop is not AI-specific.

AI Slop is only the most visible recent form of a broader cost-externalization pattern.

Human Slop, Authority Slop, Academic Slop, Political Slop, Religious Slop, Corporate Slop, Journalistic Slop, and Platform Slop can all occur when low-cost production externalizes verification, correction, re-entry, and restabilization costs to others.

## The central reversal

The question is not:

```text
Was this generated by AI?
```

The question is:

```text
Who produced it cheaply, and who must pay to verify, correct, contextualize, or restabilize it?
```

## AI Slop as visibility shock

AI did not invent Slop.

AI reduced execution cost, increased production speed, and made the old structure visible at scale.

## Human Slop as older structure

Humans have long produced Slop through:

- unsupported authority claims,
- fluent but empty commentary,
- political slogans,
- moral overstatement,
- religious certainty without re-entry,
- academic jargon without trace,
- corporate messaging that shifts repair costs,
- journalistic speculation that increases correction burden,
- SEO content that pollutes discovery terrain.

## Key sentence

```text
AI Slop is the visible shock. Human Slop is the older structure. SDeltaPhi-65 audits the cost asymmetry common to both.
```

## Correction

Do not use Slop as an anti-AI stigma label.

Use Slop as a cost-externalization audit.
