# Do-Not-Use Conditions and Common Misreadings

## Do not use SDeltaPhi-65 to claim all AI output is Slop

AI generation is not the Slop criterion.

## Do not use SDeltaPhi-65 to exempt human-created content

Human authorship is not automatic quality, authenticity, or responsibility.

## Do not treat Slop as a mere aesthetic label

Slop is not just bad writing, ugly images, or generic style.

## Do not use Slop as a stigma label without audit

Do not label content Slop without inspecting verification cost, correction burden, re-entry path, and restabilization cost.

## Do not treat low-status authors as Slop

Slop is a cost structure, not a class or status label.

## Do not treat authority as proof of non-Slop

Authority can increase audit cost and create Authority Slop.

## Do not ignore fiction and low-stakes contexts

Clearly fictional or low-stakes output may have low Slop risk if it does not externalize verification or restabilization costs.

## Do not use SDeltaPhi-65 as an anti-AI blanket argument

The module audits cost externalization, not source identity.

## Do not use SDeltaPhi-65 to avoid source-specific analysis

Different outputs require different source, trace, method, context, and re-entry audits.
