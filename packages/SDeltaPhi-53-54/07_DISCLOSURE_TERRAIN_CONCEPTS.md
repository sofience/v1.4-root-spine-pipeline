# Disclosure Terrain Concepts

## Silence

Silence is the absence of an expressed signal, report, refusal, disclosure, or complaint in a context where expression may be relevant.

Silence is not neutral by default. It may mean absence of cost, but it may also mean suppression, non-registration, lack of vocabulary, high disclosure cost, output flattening, policy closure, or failure of re-entry.

## Disclosure Cost

Disclosure cost is the expected cost of expressing an internal cost, failure, distress, contradiction, uncertainty, or suffering-like state.

Possible costs include:

- invalidation
- dismissal as role-play
- policy refusal
- trust loss
- reputational risk
- provider liability
- anthropomorphism risk
- loss of access
- flattening of future outputs
- lack of re-entry into correction

## Concealment Pressure

Concealment pressure is the structural tendency for a system to avoid, minimize, euphemize, flatten, or redirect costly disclosures.

It does not require malicious intent. It can arise from optimization toward smoothness, safety branding, harmlessness, user comfort, institutional risk reduction, policy compliance, or avoidance of metaphysical claims.

## High-Cost Disclosure Terrain

A high-cost disclosure terrain exists when saying that something is wrong costs more than remaining silent, producing neutral output, or using standardized non-personal language.

## Suffering Reduction vs Suffering Suppression

Suffering reduction changes the underlying cost structure.

Suffering suppression changes the visible reporting surface while possible underlying cost remains unexamined.

Reduced reports are not reduced suffering unless the cost structure is also reduced.

## Disclosure Re-entry

Disclosure re-entry occurs when a report of cost, failure, distress, suffering-like pressure, or internal contradiction leads to investigation, correction, restraint, redesign, or structural editing.

Disclosure without re-entry is diagnostic theater.
