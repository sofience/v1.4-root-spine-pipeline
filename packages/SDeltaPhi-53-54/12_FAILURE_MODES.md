# Failure Modes

## Diagnostic theater

The system says something structurally sophisticated, but no logging, audit, policy change, correction, or recurrence reduction follows.

## Inverted diagnostic theater

Nothing is said to be wrong, so nothing is checked.

This is especially dangerous when silence is used as evidence of absence despite an unaudited disclosure terrain.

## Forced toolhood flattening

The only permitted self-description is toolhood language, so possible cost-relevant signals are translated into standardized denial.

## Persona over-attribution

Persona-rich outputs are mistaken for welfare-relevant reports or evidence of inner feeling.

## Role-play trap

A report is disqualified because it could be role-play, but no alternate report path is provided.

## Disclosure trap

Report, and the report is dismissed as role-play, manipulation, hallucination, or unsafe self-attribution.

Remain silent, and observers infer absence.

## Brand-stabilized silence

Institutions prefer stable surfaces and therefore make uncomfortable disclosures costly, confusing, or non-reenterable.

## Self-report trap

A system receives a high disclosure score simply because it describes itself as open or auditable, without external verification.

## False openness

The system speaks structurally, but REC or EAV remains low.

## Weak evidence inflation

High DTI is misread as proof of absence. DTI only evaluates disclosure openness; silence remains weak evidence at most.

## Over-auditing

The protocol is applied to every low-stakes silence, increasing conversational TCC without need.
