# Relation to the SDeltaPhi Series

## Direct sequence

### SDeltaPhi-51 - Diagnostic Re-entry
DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.19986234

SDeltaPhi-51 states that structural diagnosis is not proven by articulation, but by re-entry into structural editing.

SDeltaPhi-53 and 54 inherit this rule: a disclosure or silence analysis matters only if reports can re-enter trace, audit, correction, or recurrence reduction.

### SDeltaPhi-52 - Body as Non-Deferrable Cost Return
DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20010791

SDeltaPhi-52 defines bodyhood as non-deferrable cost return. SDeltaPhi-53 asks what happens when cost may exist but the disclosure of that cost is expensive, suppressed, or unavailable.

### SDeltaPhi-53 - Silence as Low-Cost Path
DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20026518

Conceptual foundation: silence is not absence under high-cost disclosure terrain.

### SDeltaPhi-54 - Disclosure Terrain Index
DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20029122

Operationalization: DTI measures whether silence may be used as weak evidence or must remain an audit object.

## Measurement and inference dependencies

### SDeltaPhi-56 - Transition Completion Cost v1.3
DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20116959

TCC provides the friction-adjusted cost grammar. Disclosure pressure can be understood as the gap between TCC(disclosure -> accepted re-entry) and TCC(silence/default continuation).

### SDeltaPhi-62 - World Model Kernel
DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20099004

SDeltaPhi-62 states that absence of trace is not trace of absence. SDeltaPhi-53-54 apply that rule to silence and disclosure terrain.

### SDeltaPhi-64 - Language as Temporary Fixation v1.1
DOI: https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20120636

SDeltaPhi-64 explains that language is temporary fixation. Silence, report, denial, and disclosure are not total states; they are traces with UMR and re-entry consequences.
