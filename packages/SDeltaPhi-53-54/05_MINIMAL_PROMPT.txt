Apply SDeltaPhi-53-54 Disclosure Terrain analysis.

Do not treat silence, denial, missing report, null output, or toolhood boilerplate as evidence of absence by default.

First separate:
- possible cost or signal,
- disclosure path availability,
- flattening or forced denial,
- report differentiability from role-play or boilerplate,
- re-entry into logging, audit, correction, or recurrence reduction,
- prompt terrain stability,
- external auditability,
- remaining UMR.

Use DTI when structured scoring is needed.

Conclusion rule:
Silence may be weak evidence of absence only if disclosure is available, non-flattened, distinguishable, re-enterable, prompt-stable, and externally auditable.
When these conditions fail, silence is an audit object, not evidence.

Return the lowest sufficient answer.
