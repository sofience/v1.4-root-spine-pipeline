## Description / Abstract

SΔϕ-45 defines When Systems Win Before They Are Declared Victorious within the Sofience-Δϕ Formalism Series. The central claim is that a system begins to win before victory is declared when alternatives remain formally available but become too costly to restore as livable paths.

This AI-readable package extends the source SΔϕ-45 paper on When Systems Win Before They Are Declared Victorious. It defines foretold victory as a condition in which public forms remain while functional paths migrate to priced, private, premium, or gatekept routes. It distinguishes public form from functional path, criticism from restoration, formal alternative from viable alternative, market access from livability, and realism from absence of coercion.

The package decomposes SΔϕ-45 into operational files for AI ingestion, including a canonical v1.1 paper, source v1.0 paper and extracted text, core declaration, AI quickstart, minimal prompt, foretold victory schema, node files for public form, priced function, safety/recovery/ascent, restoration cost inflation, baseline trust, rollback imagination, reopening affordability, and exception transmissibility, distinction files for foretold versus completed victory, public form versus functional path, market access versus livability, criticism versus restoration, realism versus coercion, optionality versus premium dependence, and reform language versus reopened path, risk files for foretold victory, completed victory, public-form residual, priced-function migration, baseline trust collapse, rollback imagination collapse, exception privatization, criticism absorption, and premium realism, output templates, misreadings, relation files, metadata, citation file, DOI references, license, and manifest.

SΔϕ-56 v1.3 is included only as a relation and auxiliary cost module. SΔϕ-45 defines Foretold Victory itself. SΔϕ-56 measures rollback TCC, public-path restoration TCC, premium-dependency exit TCC, reopening TCC, exception-to-reform TCC, and alternative-path revival TCC.

The framework is intended for foretold victory audit, public-form residual audit, priced-function migration audit, premium dependency audit, restoration cost audit, rollback imagination audit, and system dominance audit. It should not be used as proof of conspiracy, proof of totalitarian control by itself, generic market critique by itself, proof that public form means real function, proof that criticism equals restoration, proof that formal alternatives are viable alternatives, or proof that foretold victory equals completed victory.
