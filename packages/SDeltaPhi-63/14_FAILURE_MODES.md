# Failure Modes

## 1. Total worldview capture

The converted module becomes a total worldview.

Correction: restore scope and activation conditions.

## 2. Theory flattening

A deep theory becomes a crude keyword detector.

Correction: preserve root proposition, history, and UMR.

## 3. Domain erasure

Historical, religious, philosophical, or political context is removed.

Correction: preserve domain limits and expertise needs.

## 4. Overgeneralization

The same theory is applied to every input.

Correction: require input trace and activation conditions.

## 5. Counterexample absorption

Counterexamples become further proof of the theory.

Correction: require falsifiability or revision path.

## 6. Module overbinding

Conditional output is treated as final conclusion.

Correction: state output strength and UMR.

## 7. UMR deletion

The theory's unexplained residue disappears.

Correction: preserve UMR explicitly.

## 8. Ideology capture

The module becomes a belief system instead of an audit tool.

Correction: route to other modules.

## 9. False equivalence

Different theories are treated as the same because their modules look similar.

Correction: preserve root proposition and distinct activation conditions.

## 10. No downstream routing

The conversion ends without routing to CASI, ACOI, USSI, God-Gui, TCC, or other needed modules.

Correction: add downstream modules.

## 11. AI convenience flattening

The AI simplifies the theory until its core tension is removed.

Correction: include blind spots and failure modes.

## 12. Hostile reduction

A theory is converted only to attack it.

Correction: identify what it detects well before listing failure modes.

## 13. Adoption bias

The AI treats a theory sympathetically and overextends it.

Correction: inspect hidden costs and overbinding.

## 14. Debunking bias

The AI treats conversion as refutation.

Correction: separate operational usefulness from truth adoption.
