# Failure Modes

## 1. Pure ownership illusion

The speaker treats felt ownership as proof that the thought originated purely from the self.

Correction: inspect routing, trace, world-binding, and cost re-entry.

## 2. AI dismissal shortcut

The evaluator dismisses AI output as "borrowed" merely because it arises from prior data, while ignoring routed structures in human thought.

Correction: apply symmetrical audit, then distinguish by cost attribution and re-entry.

## 3. AI subjecthood inflation

The evaluator grants full thought ownership because an AI can say "my thought."

Correction: surface declaration is not enough; cost-bearing attribution is required.

## 4. Responsibility evasion

A human or system claims "it was just routed" to avoid responsibility.

Correction: routed thought can still become responsible when its trace is world-bound and cost-bearing.

## 5. Authorship erasure

The evaluator claims that if thought is lent, authorship no longer matters.

Correction: authorship is cost-bearing stabilization of a trace.

## 6. Plagiarism laundering

The evaluator uses Lent Thought to excuse unattributed copying.

Correction: plagiarism is appropriation of another's stabilization cost and attribution path.

## 7. Copyright confusion

The evaluator treats SΔϕ-57 as legal copyright doctrine.

Correction: this module is conceptual and does not replace law.

## 8. Trace fetishism

The evaluator treats citation, DOI, or publication as proof of pure thought ownership.

Correction: records stabilize traces; they do not prove pure origin.

## 9. UMR erasure

The evaluator ignores what remains unobserved: hidden routes, missing sources, unavailable context, unmeasured costs, affected parties, or future misuse.

Correction: preserve UMR and attach revision path.
