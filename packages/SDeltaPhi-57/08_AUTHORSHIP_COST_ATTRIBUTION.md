# Authorship as Cost Attribution

SΔϕ-57 redefines authorship as cost-bearing stabilization of a trace.

## Core declaration

Authorship is not ownership of pure thought.

Authorship is the acceptance of cost-attribution for a trace one has stabilized into the world.

## What authorship includes

A thought may be borrowed, routed, influenced, translated, co-generated, or reassembled. Authorship begins when an interpretive position stabilizes that routed thought into a trace and accepts the cost of that trace.

This may include:

- organizing the thought,
- naming it,
- expressing it in stable form,
- publishing it,
- accepting critique,
- correcting misunderstandings,
- maintaining versions,
- citing prior traces,
- responding to misuse,
- bearing reputational cost,
- permitting future re-entry.
