# Do-Not-Use Conditions and Common Misreadings

## Do not use SΔϕ-57 to claim that all thoughts are the same

Lent Thought does not erase differences between human thought and AI output.

## Do not use SΔϕ-57 to deny human responsibility

If human thought is routed, responsibility does not disappear. It becomes cost-bearing attribution.

## Do not use SΔϕ-57 to grant AI full subjecthood by declaration

An AI saying "my thought" is not sufficient. The issue is whether cost can re-enter the system's future transition conditions.

## Do not use SΔϕ-57 as legal copyright advice

The copyright module is conceptual. It does not replace jurisdiction-specific law.

## Do not use SΔϕ-57 to excuse plagiarism

If anything, SΔϕ-57 makes plagiarism sharper: plagiarism appropriates another's stabilization cost and attribution path.

## Do not use SΔϕ-57 to erase creativity

Routed thought can still be creative. Creativity may occur through recombination, stabilization, expression, naming, and cost-bearing trace formation.

## Do not use SΔϕ-57 to reduce human thought to "just data"

Human thought may be routed, but it is often thickly world-bound through body, memory, relation, law, social position, vulnerability, mortality, and future self-narration.

## Do not use SΔϕ-57 to claim authorship is meaningless

Authorship becomes more precise: it is the acceptance of cost-attribution for a trace stabilized into the world.
