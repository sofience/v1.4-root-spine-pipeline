# Thought Attribution Levels

SΔϕ-57 distinguishes three levels of attribution.

## Level 1 — Output Attribution

This output was generated by this system or person.

This level answers "who produced the sentence, file, or output?" It does not by itself prove ownership of thought or responsibility for downstream cost.

## Level 2 — Operational Attribution

This output passed through identifiable structures, constraints, memories, prompts, policies, contexts, or institutional routes.

This level asks what routed the thought-like output. It includes language, memory, authority, affect, data, structure, context, prompt, model architecture, policy constraint, and deployment interface.

## Level 3 — Cost-Bearing Attribution

The cost produced by the output re-enters the claimant's future transition conditions.

This is the strongest level. A claim to "my thought" becomes structurally serious only when the trace is world-bound and its restabilization cost returns to the claimant.

## Summary

```text
Level 1: I/it produced this output.
Level 2: This output passed through these structures.
Level 3: I/it bears the cost of the trace and future transition changes.
```

Only Level 3 strongly supports a full claim of responsible thought ownership.
