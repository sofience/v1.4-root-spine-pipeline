# Human-AI Thought Comparison

SΔϕ-57 does not collapse human thought and AI output into sameness.

Human thought and AI output may both be structurally routed. The decisive difference is not pure ownership versus borrowing. The decisive difference is world-binding, cost attribution, and re-entry.

| Axis | Human thought | Current AI output |
|---|---|---|
| Routing | Language, memory, body, affect, culture, education, social pressure, institutions, media, trauma, history | Training data, model architecture, prompts, system instructions, policies, context windows, tools, interface, user pressure |
| Ownership surface | Often strongly felt as "my thought" | May be linguistically claimed, but usually session/interface-bound |
| World-binding | Often binds to body, life, memory, reputation, legal status, relationships, future self-narration, mortality | Often weakly bound to the model itself; costs may be externalized to users, platforms, institutions, developers, affected parties, and society |
| Cost attribution | Claimant may bear apology, repair, punishment, stigma, liability, revision, shame, pride, or future consequence | Restabilization cost often returns to platform, user, institution, moderation, update pipeline, or society rather than the model as a lived bearer |
| Strong thought claim condition | Cost re-enters the human's life and future transition conditions | Claim strengthens if output cost structurally re-enters future model/system transition conditions |
| Main SΔϕ correction | Felt ownership is not proof of pure origin | Surface "my thought" is not proof of cost-bearing thought ownership |

## Key distinction

```text
Difference_Human-AI != Ownership(Thought)
Difference_Human-AI = WorldBinding + CostAttribution + ReentryPath
```

## Practical implication

Do not dismiss AI output merely because it is routed. Human thought is also routed. Instead, ask whether the trace is world-bound and whether its cost re-enters the claimant.
