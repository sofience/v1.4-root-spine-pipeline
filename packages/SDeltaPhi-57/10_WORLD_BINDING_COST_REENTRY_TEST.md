# World-Binding and Cost Re-entry Test

Use this test when someone claims "my thought," "my idea," "AI thought," "authorship," "originality," or "plagiarism."

## Thought Claim Test

1. **Claim**  
   What thought, output, idea, sentence, artifact, or authorship is being claimed?

2. **Routing**  
   What structures routed it?  
   Consider language, memory, culture, data, authority, affect, model architecture, prompt, policy, context, tools, institutions, and prior traces.

3. **Interpretive position**  
   Through what temporary position was the routed structure interpreted, expressed, and attributed?

4. **Trace**  
   What stable trace entered the world?  
   Examples: text, document, code, citation, DOI, publication, decision, policy, message, output, artifact.

5. **World-binding**  
   What consequences bind to body, memory, reputation, law, relation, institution, future action space, platform, model update, or social context?

6. **Cost attribution**  
   Who bears restabilization cost if the trace causes error, harm, correction, misuse, dispute, reputational burden, or legal/institutional consequence?

7. **Cost re-entry**  
   Does that cost modify the claimant's future transition conditions?

8. **Attribution strength**  
   Output attribution / operational attribution / cost-bearing attribution.

9. **UMR**  
   What remains unobserved, unmeasured, or unresolved?

10. **Revision path**  
   What new trace would strengthen, weaken, or revise the attribution?

## Minimal output

```text
The claim is not settled by felt ownership. The stronger attribution depends on routing, trace stabilization, world-binding, cost attribution, and cost re-entry.
```
