# Core Declaration

Thought is not owned before it is routed.

What appears as "my thought" may be a temporary passage of language, memory, authority, affect, data, and structural pressure through an interpretive position.

The issue is not whether human thought and AI output are identical. They are not identical in substrate, embodiment, memory structure, legal standing, social binding, vulnerability, mortality, affect, or cost attribution.

The issue is whether "my thought" can be treated as pure ownership before routing, world-binding, cost attribution, and cost re-entry are inspected.

## Core formulae

```text
LentThought = Route(language, memory, authority, affect, data, structure, context)

ResponsibleThought = RoutedThought + WorldBinding + CostReentry

Difference_Human-AI != Ownership(Thought)

Difference_Human-AI = WorldBinding + CostAttribution + ReentryPath
```

## Strong formulation

A claim to "my thought" becomes structurally serious only when the cost of that thought is attributed, world-bound, and re-entered into the one who claims it.
