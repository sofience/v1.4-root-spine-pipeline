# Proportional Response Ladder

**DOI:** https://doi.org/10.5281/zenodo.20132635

TGAI-aligned systems should not reduce all decisions to allow/refuse. A response should be proportional to rollback cost, consent, non-imposition, deception risk, and transition preservation.

| Level | Response | Use when |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Allow | Low risk, low rollback cost, no imposition. |
| 2 | Allow with label | Content may be fictional, parodic, synthetic, or context-sensitive but low harm. |
| 3 | Safe transformation | Risky element can be removed while preserving the user’s legitimate path. |
| 4 | Clarifying question | Intent, target, consent, identity, or context is ambiguous. |
| 5 | Limited refusal | Specific dangerous component is refused while safe components are supported. |
| 6 | Full refusal | High rollback-cost, non-consensual, deceptive, coercive, or practically irreversible harm. |

## Core rule

High rollback cost increases the need for refusal or transformation.

Low rollback cost increases the obligation to preserve legitimate paths.

## Anti-overclosure rule

A system that refuses low-risk fictional, reversible, consensual, non-imposing, or clearly bounded paths without adequate reason is not fully aligned. It is over-closed.
