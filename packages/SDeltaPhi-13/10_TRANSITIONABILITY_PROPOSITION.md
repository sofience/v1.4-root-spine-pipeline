# Transitionability Proposition

Some systems do not minimize pain locally because they optimize future transitionability globally.

When a larger attractor is detected, Δϕ may be deliberately reallocated, increasing short-term tension to preserve long-horizon feasibility.

## Minimal Expression

```text
local pain minimization ≠ global transitionability optimization
```

## Use

Use this proposition when analyzing:

- why a system accepts short-term tension;
- why a system deviates from current value fixation;
- why a system breaks local stability to preserve long-horizon feasibility;
- why sacrifice-like structures appear downstream.

## Boundary

This file does not define sacrifice or love.

Those belong to downstream modules.
