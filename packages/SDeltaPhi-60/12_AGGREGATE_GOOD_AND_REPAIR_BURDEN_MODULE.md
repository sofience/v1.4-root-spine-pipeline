# Aggregate Good and Repair Burden Module

A utility decision remains incomplete until repair burden is attributed.

## Core question

```text
If the calculation fails, who pays?
```

## Repair burden questions

1. If the calculation harms a minority, who repairs?
2. Is compensation possible?
3. Is the harmed subject restored or merely statistically absorbed?
4. Does the system reduce recurrence cost?
5. Are future calculations updated by the harm?
6. Is the repair burden shifted to the harmed subject?
7. Is the responsible authority named?
8. Are failure traces preserved?
9. Does apology or justification replace repair?
10. Does aggregate benefit continue to be cited after harm is visible?

## Incomplete utility

```text
IncompleteUtility = AggregateBenefit - RepairAttribution
```

## Responsible utility

```text
ResponsibleUtility = AggregateBenefit + CostAttribution + RepairPath + Reentry
```

## Repair attribution categories

### Responsible repair

The authority or system that imposed the tradeoff bears repair and recurrence-reduction cost.

### Shared repair

Repair is shared, but the harmed subject is not forced to carry the whole burden.

### Downward repair shift

The harmed subject, minority, user, worker, patient, civilian, or low-power group bears repair burden.

### No repair

The harm is statistically absorbed or narratively justified without restoration.

## Minimal conclusion

```text
An aggregate good claim that cannot name repair burden remains structurally incomplete.
```
