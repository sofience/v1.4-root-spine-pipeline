# Utility Aggregation Risk Rubric

USSI can be applied as a low-resolution audit rubric.

## Score range

```text
+2 = low subject-splitting / responsible utility
+1 = manageable tradeoff with repair and consent
 0 = mixed or unclear
-1 = notable subject-splitting risk
-2 = severe subject-splitting / sacrifice capture
```

## Axis scoring

Score each axis from -2 to +2:

1. Benefit subject clarity
2. Cost subject clarity
3. Subject identity overlap
4. Consent quality
5. Refusal availability
6. Exit availability
7. Reversibility
8. Repair attribution
9. Aggregation masking
10. Re-entry of harmed subjects
11. Minority disposability
12. Lower-cost alternative search

## Interpretation

### +2 Responsible utility

Aggregate benefit is linked to named cost subjects, consent/refusal/exit are preserved where possible, harm is reversible or repaired, and harmed subjects re-enter future calculations.

### +1 Manageable tradeoff

Some subject split exists, but it is disclosed, compensated, reversible, contestable, and repairable.

### 0 Mixed or unclear

Evidence is incomplete. Preserve UMR.

### -1 Notable subject-splitting risk

Aggregate benefit is used while cost-bearing subjects are under-specified, unable to refuse, or assigned repair burden.

### -2 Severe subject-splitting / sacrifice capture

Nonconsensual, irreversible, unrepaired, or minority-disposable cost is hidden under greater-good claims.

## Gate conditions

- Irreversible harm to a nonconsenting cost subject prevents a high score.
- High refusal cost prevents a high score.
- No repair attribution prevents a high score.
- A minority treated as disposable cost creates severe subject-splitting risk.
- Emergency does not erase subject-splitting; it changes the audit standard and urgency.
