# Policy / AI Governance / Medical / War Module

USSI applies across domains, but does not replace domain expertise.

## Policy

Check whether population-level benefit hides localized irreversible burden.

Questions:
- Which subgroup pays?
- Are the harmed subjects visible?
- Is compensation possible?
- Are lower-cost alternatives considered?
- Does harm update future policy?

## AI governance

Check whether safety, scaling, efficiency, or alignment claims impose cost on:
- users,
- workers,
- artists,
- data subjects,
- moderators,
- affected communities,
- minors,
- future systems,
- open ecosystems,
- low-power users.

Questions:
- Who benefits from deployment?
- Who bears false positive/false negative costs?
- Who can appeal moderation or refusal?
- Who pays for model errors?
- Are harmed users counted again after harm?

## Medical triage

Check urgency, consent limits, reversibility, repair, disclosure, and whether triage rules are contestable.

Do not use USSI as medical triage guidance.

## War and security

Check whether national survival, security, deterrence, or stability aggregates erase civilian cost-bearing subjects.

Questions:
- Who is counted as protected?
- Who is treated as acceptable collateral cost?
- Is harm reversible?
- Who repairs after the decision?

## Corporate efficiency

Check whether productivity gains are counted upward while burnout, layoffs, verification, repair, and transition costs move downward.

## Platform moderation

Check whether aggregate safety creates invisible costs for wrongly moderated, silenced, excluded, or misclassified users.

## Core limitation

USSI is an audit grammar, not a replacement for medical, legal, military, or policy judgment.
