# Core Declaration

Utility is not rejected.

But utility must be audited when the subject who receives benefit is not the same as the subject who bears cost.

Utilitarian Subject-Splitting occurs when a system counts benefit at the aggregate level while assigning harm, sacrifice, repair burden, silence, or irreversibility to separable individuals or subgroups.

The question is not only:

```text
Does this maximize total benefit?
```

The question is:

```text
Who is split into the cost-bearing position, and can that subject refuse, exit, repair, or re-enter the calculation?
```

## Minimal formulae

```text
SubjectSplitting = BenefitSubject != CostSubject

AggregateMasking = TotalBenefit - VisibleIndividualCost

SacrificeCapture = GreaterGoodClaim + NonConsensualCostTransfer

IrreversibleMinorCost = SmallAggregateCostClaim + HighIrreversibilityForMinority

RepairBurdenShift = PolicyBenefit -> Aggregate ; RepairCost -> HarmedSubject

ConsentFailure = CostImposition - RefusalPath - ExitPath

ResponsibleUtility = AggregateBenefit + CostAttribution + RepairPath + Reentry
```

## Strong formulation

The aggregate good is incomplete until the cost-bearing subject is named, the cost is attributed, refusal and exit are inspected, repair burden is assigned, and harmed subjects can re-enter future calculation.
