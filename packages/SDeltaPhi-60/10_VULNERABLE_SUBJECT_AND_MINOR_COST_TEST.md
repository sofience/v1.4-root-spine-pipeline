# Vulnerable Subject and Minor Cost Test

The most dangerous utility claims often describe costs as small at the aggregate level while those costs are irreversible for specific subjects.

## Minor cost test

1. Is the cost described as small at the aggregate level?
2. Is it irreversible for the affected subject?
3. Is the affected subject vulnerable?
4. Is the cost repeated across many weak subjects?
5. Is the cost hard to disclose?
6. Is repair unavailable?
7. Is the harm normalized as statistical noise?
8. Is the harmed subject re-counted after harm?
9. Is the subgroup treated as acceptable loss?
10. Are local collapses hidden by global improvement?

## Vulnerable subject indicators

- low power,
- low visibility,
- high dependence,
- limited exit,
- low audit access,
- high disclosure cost,
- minority status,
- disability,
- poverty,
- age,
- captivity,
- platform dependence,
- medical dependence,
- legal precarity,
- informational disadvantage.

## Core warning

```text
A cost that is small for the aggregate can be an irreversible world collapse for the subject who bears it.
```

## Minimal conclusion

```text
Do not treat statistically small harm as morally or operationally small until the affected subject's reversibility, repair, refusal, and re-entry are audited.
```
