## Core purpose

SΔϕ-60 introduces the **Utilitarian Subject-Splitting Index (USSI)** as an audit module for utilitarian reasoning, greater-good claims, sacrifice logic, efficiency claims, policy tradeoffs, AI governance, population-level optimization, medical triage, war/security decisions, and platform-scale moderation.

The package does not reject utility reasoning. It audits whether utility reasoning separates the subject who receives aggregate benefit from the subject who bears concrete cost, harm, repair burden, silence, or irreversibility.

## Core declaration

Utility is not rejected.

But utility must be audited when the subject who receives benefit is not the same as the subject who bears cost.

Utilitarian Subject-Splitting occurs when a system counts benefit at the aggregate level while assigning harm, sacrifice, repair burden, silence, or irreversibility to separable individuals or subgroups.

The question is not only:

```text
Does this maximize total benefit?
```

The question is:

```text
Who is split into the cost-bearing position, and can that subject refuse, exit, repair, or re-enter the calculation?
```

- general moral score,
- automatic rejection of emergency reasoning.
