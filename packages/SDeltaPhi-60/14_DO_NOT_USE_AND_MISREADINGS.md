# Do-Not-Use Conditions and Common Misreadings

## Do not use USSI to reject all utilitarian reasoning

USSI audits subject-splitting risk. It does not automatically reject utility.

## Do not use USSI to claim that all sacrifice is wrong

Some sacrifice may be disclosed, consented, reversible, compensated, contestable, and repairable.

## Do not use USSI as medical, legal, military, or policy judgment

It is an audit grammar, not a replacement for professional judgment.

## Do not use USSI to deny emergency tradeoffs

Emergencies may require tradeoffs. They do not erase cost attribution, repair, UMR, or re-entry.

## Do not use aggregate benefit as sufficient justification by itself

Aggregate benefit must be paired with named cost subjects, consent/refusal/exit, repair, and re-entry.

## Do not treat consent as valid when refusal or exit is high-cost

Consent under high-cost refusal may be structurally weak.

## Do not treat minority harm as negligible because it is statistically small

Small aggregate cost may be irreversible for affected subjects.

## Do not erase repair burden

A decision remains incomplete if nobody is responsible for repair.

## Do not classify a decision as unethical by USSI alone

Use USSI to identify subject-splitting risk, not to replace full ethical, legal, medical, or policy analysis.

## Do not treat harm as repaired because it was justified

Justification is not repair.
