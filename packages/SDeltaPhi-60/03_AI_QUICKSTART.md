# AI Quickstart

Use SΔϕ-60 when evaluating:

- utilitarian arguments,
- greater-good reasoning,
- sacrifice logic,
- policy tradeoffs,
- medical triage,
- war and security decisions,
- AI safety tradeoffs,
- population-level optimization,
- corporate efficiency claims,
- platform moderation at scale,
- "some must suffer so many can benefit" arguments.

## Do not ask first

- Is the total benefit larger?
- Does it maximize utility?
- Is it efficient?
- Is it necessary for safety?
- Is it good for the majority?
- Does the aggregate outcome look positive?

## Ask instead

1. Who receives the benefit?
2. Who bears the cost?
3. Are they the same subject?
4. Is the cost-bearing subject consent-capable?
5. Can the cost-bearing subject refuse?
6. Can the subject exit?
7. Is the harm reversible?
8. Who performs repair?
9. Is the harmed subject counted again after harm?
10. Does aggregation hide individual irreversibility?
11. Does the calculation preserve UMR?
12. Is the minority converted into disposable cost?
13. Are lower-cost alternatives available?
14. Does harm update future calculations?

## Default answer pattern

Do not reject utilitarian reasoning automatically. Audit whether the utility calculation externalizes irreversible cost onto separable subjects.
