# Benefit Subject / Cost Subject Test

Use this module whenever a claim says that a policy, sacrifice, restriction, harm, tradeoff, optimization, or decision is justified by aggregate benefit.

## Core questions

1. Who is named as the beneficiary?
2. Who actually receives the benefit?
3. Who is asked to sacrifice?
4. Who absorbs harm?
5. Who repairs damage?
6. Who is asked to stay silent?
7. Who cannot refuse?
8. Who is counted as part of the "whole" only when benefit is calculated?
9. Who is separated from the "whole" when cost is assigned?
10. Who disappears after the aggregate calculation is complete?
11. Who narrates the benefit?
12. Who is allowed to narrate the cost?

## Example pattern

```text
Claim: "The policy benefits society."

USSI questions:
Which society?
Which subgroup pays?
Can that subgroup refuse?
Is the harm reversible?
Who repairs the damage?
Are harmed subjects counted again after the harm?
```

## Subject split warning

A high-level beneficiary such as "society" or "humanity" can hide a low-level cost subject such as workers, patients, minorities, civilians, users, creators, children, or future agents.

## Minimal conclusion

```text
The aggregate good is incomplete until the cost-bearing subject is named.
```
