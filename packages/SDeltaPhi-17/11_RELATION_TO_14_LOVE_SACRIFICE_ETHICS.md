# Relation to SDeltaPhi-14 Love, Sacrifice, and Ethics

SDeltaPhi-14 defines love as irreversible coupling and shared attractor formation.

Sacrifice redistributes transition weight from self-stability toward shared stability.

Ethics discriminates real shared attractors from phantom shared attractors.

## Love label risk

The phrase:

```text
"I do this because I love you."
```

may bypass the real/phantom attractor test.

## Audit

Ask:

1. Is the shared attractor real?
2. Is it phantom?
3. Is the sacrifice mutual?
4. Who bears the transition-weight redistribution?
5. Does the label lower or raise the other's editability?
6. Does the label justify control?

## Formula

```text
LoveLabelAbuse =
"love" label
+ bypass(real/phantom attractor discrimination)
+ sacrifice cost externalization
```

## Minimal rule

Love is not proven by the word "love."

Love requires relational persistence and shared-attractor validation.
