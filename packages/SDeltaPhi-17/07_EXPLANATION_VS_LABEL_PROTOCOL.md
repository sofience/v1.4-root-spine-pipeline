# Explanation vs Label Protocol

A label is not an explanation.

A label may interpret an explanation, but it may not replace explanation.

## Explanation asks

```text
What happened?
What path was selected?
What cost was assigned?
Who bears the cost?
What changed?
What can be revised?
What would count as correction?
Who can re-enter the path?
```

## Label examples

```text
"This is sacred."
"This is mystery."
"This is truth."
"This is good."
"This is evil."
"This is love."
"This is human."
"This is the nation."
```

These may have interpretive meaning.

But they are not explanations by themselves.

## Audit

When a label appears, ask:

1. What does the label explain?
2. What does it hide?
3. What cost does it assign?
4. Who benefits from the label?
5. Who pays because of the label?
6. Does it preserve validation?
7. Does it preserve re-entry?
8. Does it preserve editability?

## Minimal conclusion

```text
The label may be meaningful, but it does not yet explain the operation.
```
