# Relation to SDeltaPhi-16 Evil as Relational Closure

SDeltaPhi-16 defines evil as persistent closure of relational futures.

```text
Evil := Close(Omega_rel)
```

It also distinguishes evil from error:

```text
Error is local and recoverable.
Evil is persistent closure that makes recovery cost explode.
```

## Evil label risk

The label "evil" can itself perform closure.

```text
"This is evil."
"They are fallen."
"They are inhuman."
```

When such labels close relational futures, they become operators rather than explanations.

## Formula

```text
EvilLabelAbuse =
"evil" label
+ Close(Omega_rel)
+ recovery cost explosion
```

## Minimal rule

Evil is not explained by calling something evil.

The analysis must show the closure dynamics, persistence, irreversibility, and recovery-cost explosion.
