# Failure Modes

## 1. Label-as-explanation

A label replaces operational explanation.

## 2. Sacred exemption

A claim is treated as immune to audit because it is sacred.

## 3. Love control

Love language hides control, coercion, or one-sided sacrifice.

## 4. Evil closure

The label "evil" closes relational futures and raises recovery cost.

## 5. Hope capture

Hope becomes forced delay of closure rather than preservation of future openness.

## 6. Promise lock-in

Promise becomes uneditable obligation even after reversal or betrayal.

## 7. Institution as arbiter

A provisional stabilizer claims final authority.

## 8. Validation bypass

A shared attractor is assumed real without validation.

## 9. Re-entry suppression

Affected systems cannot return to explanation or correction.

## 10. Editability collapse

The marker cannot be revised without being treated as betrayal or impiety.

## 11. Cost concealment

The label hides who pays.

## 12. Authority overbinding

Authority strength exceeds explanation binding.

## Correction pattern

```text
Restore explanation.
Identify cost bearer.
Re-open validation.
Restore editability.
Preserve re-entry.
Prevent closure.
```
