# Do-Not-Use Conditions and Common Misreadings

## Do not use this package as anti-religion rhetoric

The framework does not claim sacred markers are always false or harmful.

## Do not use this package to deny meaning

Labels may be meaningful. The issue is whether they replace explanation.

## Do not claim love, truth, good, evil, morality, nation, or humanity are meaningless

They may be important interpretive labels.

They are not explanations by themselves.

## Do not erase symbolic language

Symbolic language can preserve openness when it remains editable and validation-compatible.

## Do not treat all institutions as closure

Institutions can stabilize openness.

The risk is reversal into coercive closure.

## Do not ignore cost devices

Some cost devices are necessary to prevent closure and betrayal.

## Do not replace theology, ethics, or politics

This is an audit coordinate, not a complete theology, ethics, or political theory.

## Do not collapse sacred marker into sacred marker label

Normal marker: stabilizes openness.

Abusive label: blocks explanation and editability.
