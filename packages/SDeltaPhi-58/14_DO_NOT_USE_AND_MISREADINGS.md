# Do-Not-Use Conditions and Common Misreadings

## Do not use CASI to label a group as cult, scam, evil, abusive, or illegitimate by score alone

CASI is a cost-attribution audit module, not a label generator.

## Do not use CASI as legal judgment

CASI does not replace law, investigation, evidence, due process, or professional assessment.

## Do not use CASI as theological truth detector

CASI does not determine whether a doctrine is true. It audits cost attribution around doctrine and authority.

## Do not use CASI as political ideology classifier

CASI can audit political movements, but does not decide ideology by name alone.

## Do not treat all asymmetry as bad

Some asymmetry may be legitimate when disclosed, compensated, reversible, contestable, and repairable.

## Do not treat internal satisfaction as proof of symmetry

Internal harmony can coexist with high exit cost, loyalty pressure, or interpretive closure.

## Do not treat external criticism as proof of asymmetry

External hostility can distort the cost map. Preserve UMR.

## Do not ignore context

Voluntary sacrifice, emergency conditions, training roles, protective hierarchy, expertise, and care obligations may explain some asymmetry.

## Do not erase member agency

Members may choose sacrifice. Audit whether choice is informed, reversible, non-punitive, and not coerced.

## Do not attack vulnerable communities without evidence

CASI should reduce careless labeling, not enable it.

## Do not confuse mission intensity with capture

A demanding mission is not automatically abusive. Audit cost attribution.
