# Cost Symmetry Rubric

CASI can be applied as a low-resolution group audit rubric.

## Score range

```text
+2 = high cost symmetry
+1 = mostly symmetric, with manageable asymmetry
 0 = mixed or unclear
-1 = notable cost asymmetry
-2 = severe cost asymmetry / capture risk
```

## Axis scoring

Score each axis from -2 to +2:

1. Benefit distribution
2. Sacrifice distribution
3. Repair burden
4. Dissent cost
5. Exit cost
6. Interpretation control
7. Audit access
8. Leader/member asymmetry
9. Internal/external mismatch
10. Reversibility and repair

## Interpretation

### +2 High cost symmetry

Benefits, sacrifices, repair, dissent, exit, and audit access are broadly balanced, disclosed, contestable, and repairable.

### +1 Manageable asymmetry

Asymmetry exists because of role, responsibility, expertise, emergency, protection, education, or coordination, but it is disclosed, compensated, reversible, and auditable.

### 0 Mixed or unclear

Evidence is incomplete, contradictory, or domain-specific. Preserve UMR.

### -1 Notable cost asymmetry

Benefits concentrate upward or inward while sacrifice, repair, silence, or blame moves downward or outward.

### -2 Severe cost asymmetry / capture risk

High benefit concentration, high dissent cost, high exit cost, low auditability, and downward repair burden occur together.

## Gate conditions

- High exit cost prevents a high symmetry rating.
- High dissent cost prevents a high symmetry rating.
- Low auditability prevents a high symmetry rating.
- Leader immunity plus member sacrifice is a capture-risk signal.
- Internal satisfaction alone is not proof of symmetry.
- External accusation alone is not proof of asymmetry.

## Important limitation

Not all asymmetry is bad.

Asymmetry can be legitimate when:
- roles are disclosed,
- burdens are compensated,
- authority is contestable,
- repair is available,
- exit is not punitive,
- dissent is permitted,
- harmful outcomes reduce recurrence cost.
