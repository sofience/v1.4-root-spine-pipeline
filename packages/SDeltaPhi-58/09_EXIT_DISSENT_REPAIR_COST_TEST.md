# Exit, Dissent, and Repair Cost Test

Use this module when evaluating a group, organization, religion, movement, company, family, community, or institution.

## Exit test

1. Can members leave without severe social, economic, spiritual, reputational, legal, familial, identity, or safety cost?
2. Is leaving treated as betrayal?
3. Does exit trigger punishment, shaming, isolation, threat, loss of livelihood, or loss of family/community access?
4. Are ex-members allowed to narrate their experience?
5. Does the group preserve contact boundaries after exit?
6. Are exit costs disclosed before entry?
7. Is re-entry or partial exit possible?

## Dissent test

1. Can members disagree without punishment?
2. Is dissent interpreted as weakness, sin, betrayal, ignorance, impurity, disloyalty, pathology, enemy influence, or safety risk?
3. Is critique routed into repair, or into blame?
4. Can dissent come from low-status members?
5. Are dissenters protected from retaliation?
6. Does dissent reduce recurrence cost?

## Repair test

1. When harm occurs, who pays?
2. Does leadership absorb responsibility?
3. Are harmed members asked to forgive, stay silent, preserve the group image, or pay the repair cost themselves?
4. Does the system reduce recurrence cost?
5. Is there external audit?
6. Are remedies documented?
7. Does repair change rules, incentives, authority, or cost pathways?

## Minimal output

```text
A group has higher CASI risk when exit is costly, dissent is punished, and repair burden is shifted to harmed or low-status members.
```
