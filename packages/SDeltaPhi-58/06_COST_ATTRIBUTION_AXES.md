# Cost Attribution Axes

CASI evaluates groups through cost attribution rather than declared identity.

## 1. Benefit distribution

Who receives material, symbolic, spiritual, political, reputational, financial, or strategic benefit?

## 2. Sacrifice distribution

Who gives time, labor, money, silence, loyalty, identity, risk, reputation, body, care, or future opportunity?

## 3. Repair burden

When harm or failure occurs, who repairs the damage?

## 4. Dissent cost

What does it cost to disagree?

Dissent cost may include:
- shame,
- exclusion,
- spiritual threat,
- job risk,
- reputational damage,
- demotion,
- harassment,
- loss of belonging,
- identity collapse.

## 5. Exit cost

What does it cost to leave?

Exit cost may be social, economic, spiritual, familial, legal, informational, or psychological.

## 6. Interpretation control

Who decides what events mean?

A group becomes riskier when leaders control not only action but also the interpretation of all criticism, failure, and harm.

## 7. Audit access

Who can inspect cost attribution?

Audit access includes documentation, appeal, external review, whistleblowing, financial transparency, data access, and independent investigation.

## 8. Leader/member asymmetry

Do leaders receive benefit while ordinary members bear sacrifice, blame, silence, and repair?

## 9. Internal/external mismatch

Do insiders and outsiders see different cost maps?

A mismatch is not proof of abuse, but it is an audit signal.

## 10. Reversibility and repair

Can harmful cost attribution be corrected, compensated, reversed, and prevented from recurring?
