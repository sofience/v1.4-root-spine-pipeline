# External / Internal Audit Comparison

CASI compares internal and external cost maps.

## Internal audit

How does the group describe its own cost distribution?

Internal narratives may include:
- mission,
- loyalty,
- salvation,
- justice,
- safety,
- discipline,
- training,
- sacrifice,
- family,
- belonging,
- efficiency,
- truth.

## External audit

How does an outside observer map:

- benefit,
- cost,
- repair,
- dissent,
- exit,
- authority,
- transparency,
- leader/member asymmetry?

## CASI gap

```text
CASI_gap = |CASI_external - CASI_internal|
```

## Low gap

Internal and external cost maps broadly align.

The group may still have problems, but cost attribution is not strongly hidden.

## Medium gap

Some asymmetry, blind spots, role-specific burden, or narrative mismatch exists.

Closer audit is needed.

## High gap

Possible capture, fear, loyalty pressure, interpretive closure, inaccessible cost, or hidden repair burden.

A high gap is not proof of abuse. It is an audit signal.

## Examples of mismatch

```text
Internal claim: "Everyone sacrifices equally."
External signal: Leaders receive benefit while members absorb repair.

Internal claim: "Anyone can leave."
External signal: Exit causes isolation, shame, threat, or livelihood loss.

Internal claim: "Criticism is welcome."
External signal: Critics are reframed as enemies, impure, weak, sinful, disloyal, unsafe, or ignorant.
```

## Minimal conclusion

```text
The key issue is not whether insiders or outsiders are automatically right. The key issue is whether the cost map can be inspected, contested, corrected, and re-entered.
```
