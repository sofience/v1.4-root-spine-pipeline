## Core purpose

SΔϕ-58 introduces the **Cost Attribution Symmetry Index (CASI)** as a group, organization, movement, religion, company, institution, and community audit module.

The package does not label a group as good, evil, cult, scam, orthodox, heretical, democratic, or abusive by name alone.

It asks how benefit, sacrifice, repair burden, dissent cost, exit cost, and interpretive authority are attributed.

## Core declaration

A group is not evaluated first by its declared values.

A group is audited by how it attributes cost.

Cost Attribution Symmetry asks:

- Who receives benefit?
- Who bears sacrifice?
- Who controls interpretation?
- Who pays for repair?
- Who may dissent?
- Who may exit?
- Who may audit the attribution?

A group becomes structurally risky when benefit concentrates upward while cost, repair, silence, loyalty, guilt, and exit burden are pushed downward.

## Use this package when evaluating

- organizations,
- religious groups,
- political movements,
- companies,
- activist communities,
- AI governance groups,
- families or closed communities,
- fandoms or online communities,
- institutions claiming sacrifice, loyalty, mission, truth, salvation, justice, efficiency, or safety.

## Do not use this package as

- a cult label generator,
- legal judgment,
- theological truth detector,
- political ideology classifier,
- proof of bad intent,
- replacement for investigation,
- general moral score,
- attack tool against vulnerable communities.

## Operational summary

```text
CASI asks not "what does the group claim?"
but "who receives benefit, who bears cost, who repairs failure, who can dissent, who can exit, and who can audit the attribution?"
```
