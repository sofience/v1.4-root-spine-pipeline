# Core Declaration

A group is not evaluated first by its declared values.

A group is audited by how it attributes cost.

Cost Attribution Symmetry asks:

```text
Who receives benefit?
Who bears sacrifice?
Who controls interpretation?
Who pays for repair?
Who may dissent?
Who may exit?
Who may audit the attribution?
```

A group becomes structurally risky when benefit concentrates upward while cost, repair, silence, loyalty, guilt, and exit burden are pushed downward.

## Minimal formulae

```text
CostAsymmetry = Benefit_upward + Cost_downward + Repair_downward

CaptureRisk = High_asymmetry + High_exit_cost + Low_auditability

SacredCapture = Authority_claim + Cost_asymmetry + Dissent_penalty

CASI_gap = |CASI_external - CASI_internal|

RepairSymmetry = Failure_cost -> ResponsibleAuthority rather than WeakestMembers
```

## Strong formulation

The declared mission of a group is not enough. A group becomes structurally legible when its benefit, sacrifice, repair burden, dissent cost, exit cost, and interpretive authority are mapped.
