# Failure Modes

## 1. Label capture

CASI is used as a cult/scam/evil label generator instead of a cost audit.

Correction: name the cost-attribution signals instead of jumping to labels.

## 2. Symmetry absolutism

All cost asymmetry is judged bad.

Correction: evaluate disclosure, compensation, reversibility, contestability, and repair.

## 3. Mission laundering

A noble mission hides cost transfer.

Correction: audit who pays for the mission.

## 4. Sacrifice romanticization

Member suffering is reframed as loyalty, faith, growth, purification, resilience, justice, or discipline.

Correction: inspect consent, exit, repair, and benefit distribution.

## 5. Leader immunity

Leader failure is paid for by members.

Correction: track repair burden and accountability.

## 6. Exit-cost blindness

The group says members are free to leave, but actual exit cost is ignored.

Correction: audit social, economic, spiritual, legal, familial, and identity cost.

## 7. Dissent-cost blindness

The group says criticism is welcome, but critics pay hidden penalties.

Correction: audit post-dissent consequences.

## 8. Internal harmony illusion

Internal satisfaction reports hide silence, fear, loyalty pressure, or interpretive closure.

Correction: compare internal and external cost maps.

## 9. External hostility bias

External critics are dismissed as enemies, haters, sinners, corrupt, ignorant, unsafe, or outsiders.

Correction: separate critic quality from cost evidence.

## 10. Audit theater

Audit procedures exist, but no real correction, compensation, or recurrence reduction follows.

Correction: check re-entry and repair.

## 11. Agency erasure

Members' voluntary sacrifice is dismissed as false consciousness without evidence.

Correction: preserve agency while auditing cost conditions.

## 12. Sacred or mission immunity

Sacredness, salvation, justice, safety, efficiency, or progress is used to exempt cost attribution from audit.

Correction: no mission exempts cost attribution.

## 13. Outsider overreach

A weak external observer imposes a cost map without access to internal evidence.

Correction: preserve UMR and revision path.

## 14. Powerful group self-exemption

A powerful institution demands evidence standards that make cost asymmetry practically unauditable.

Correction: audit access itself is part of CASI.
