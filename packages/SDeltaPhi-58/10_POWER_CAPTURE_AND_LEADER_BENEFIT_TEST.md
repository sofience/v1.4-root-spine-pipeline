# Power Capture and Leader Benefit Test

Power capture risk increases when leaders, authorities, owners, elders, founders, experts, clergy, executives, moderators, or inner circles receive benefit while ordinary members bear sacrifice.

## Capture risk signals

- leaders receive benefit while members bear sacrifice,
- leadership error is paid for by ordinary members,
- loyalty is demanded but accountability is weak,
- criticism is reframed as betrayal,
- sacred mission justifies cost asymmetry,
- repair is performed by the harmed party,
- exit cost is high,
- audit access is low,
- leader intent is treated as proof of group safety,
- status protects leaders from ordinary consequences,
- "for the mission" rhetoric hides downward cost transfer.

## Formula

```text
CaptureRisk = BenefitConcentration + DownwardCostTransfer + ExitCost + LowAuditability
```

## Leader benefit audit

1. What benefits are concentrated?
2. Are benefits material, sexual, reputational, spiritual, political, symbolic, informational, or logistical?
3. Are benefits disclosed?
4. Are leaders accountable for failure?
5. Do leaders bear higher repair cost when their decisions cause harm?
6. Can members inspect leader benefit?
7. Can members challenge leader interpretation?
8. Can leaders be removed or corrected?

## Downward cost transfer audit

1. Who apologizes when leaders fail?
2. Who stays silent to preserve image?
3. Who absorbs burnout?
4. Who performs unpaid repair?
5. Who pays reputational cost?
6. Who is blamed for insufficient loyalty, faith, discipline, resilience, or understanding?

## Minimal conclusion

```text
CASI risk rises when authority receives benefit while lower-status members bear sacrifice, silence, repair, and exit burden.
```
