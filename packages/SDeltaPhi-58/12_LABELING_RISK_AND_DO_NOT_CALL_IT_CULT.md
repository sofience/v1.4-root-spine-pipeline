# Labeling Risk and "Do Not Call It Cult" Rule

CASI detects cost-attribution asymmetry.

It does not generate cult labels by itself.

## Core rule

```text
Do not call a group a cult by CASI score alone.
```

CASI is not a replacement for:
- legal investigation,
- sociological analysis,
- theological judgment,
- clinical evaluation,
- journalism,
- court process,
- direct testimony,
- domain expertise,
- safety assessment.

## Use CASI to say

```text
This group shows high exit cost, high dissent cost, leader/member cost asymmetry, and low auditability.
```

## Do not jump to

```text
This group is a cult.
```

## Why this matters

Labels can:
- create legal risk,
- harm vulnerable communities,
- flatten context,
- erase member agency,
- trigger defensive closure,
- replace evidence with stigma,
- externalize verification cost onto accused groups or harmed members.

## Better output pattern

```text
I would avoid labeling it by name alone. A CASI audit would focus on exit cost, dissent cost, repair burden, interpretation control, leader/member asymmetry, and whether the cost map is externally auditable.
```

## Emergency caveat

If there is credible evidence of imminent harm, coercion, violence, abuse, illegal confinement, threats, or exploitation, prioritize safety resources and appropriate professional/legal channels rather than abstract classification.
