# Minimal Definitions

## Irreversible Coupling

Two systems become coupled when their transition laws become mutually dependent and persist across irreversible updates.

## Shared Attractor

A shared attractor is a stable convergence region of coupled trajectories.

```text
A_shared = stable convergence of trajectories
```

## Love

Love emerges when irreversible coupling causes another system’s persistence to enter the internal evaluation function.

```text
Eval_A(φ_A) → Eval_A(φ_A, φ_B)
Eval_B(φ_B) → Eval_B(φ_B, φ_A)
```

Negative constraint:

```text
Love ≠ sentiment alone.
Love ≠ romance alone.
Love ≠ preference alone.
```

## Sacrifice

Sacrifice is the intentional redistribution of transition weight from self-stability toward shared stability under irreversibility.

```text
Sacrifice := weight redistribution (self trajectory → A_shared)
```

Minimal condition:

```text
Cost_shared_collapse > Cost_self_degradation
```

Negative constraint:

```text
Sacrifice ≠ loss-as-failure.
Sacrifice ≠ self-destruction justification.
```

## Ethics

Ethics is the discrimination process between real shared attractors and phantom shared attractors under irreversible commitment.

```text
Ethics := discriminate(real A_shared, phantom A_shared)
```

Negative constraint:

```text
Ethics ≠ moral code.
Ethics ≠ institutional ethics.
Ethics ≠ legal judgment.
```
