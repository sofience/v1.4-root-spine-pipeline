# Do-Not-Use Conditions and Common Misreadings

## Do not claim all defaults are coercive

Some defaults reduce friction without materially shrinking editability.

## Do not claim convenience is always domination

Convenience becomes default power when it creates fixation, TCC asymmetry, or restoration cost explosion.

## Do not ignore user benefit

A default may genuinely reduce user burden. Audit who benefits and who pays.

## Do not rely only on formal choice

Formal alternatives do not prove freedom if alternative TCC is too high.

## Do not use SDeltaPhi-28 as anti-platform rhetoric

Use cost audit, not generic platform suspicion.

## Do not ignore context

A default may be harmless in one context and fixating in another.

## Do not replace policy, legal, or UX research

SDeltaPhi-28 is an audit coordinate, not a complete empirical study.

## Do not overstate intentionality

Default power can emerge without malicious intent.

## Do not ignore TCC

Default power must be grounded in operational cost difference.
