## 1. Motivation

SΔϕ-10 fixed interpretation, learning, and inference as a single re-entry structure. This forces an ontological clarification: what are the minimal entities implied by re-entry? We fix four: world, other, self, and consciousness.

## 2. Minimal axioms

- **Axiom 1 — Re-entry primacy.** Interpretation, learning, and inference are temporal phase differences of the same Δϕ re-entry process.
- **Axiom 2 — Boundary formation.** Persistent mismatch between expected transition Δϕ̂ and actual transition Δϕ stabilizes a boundary.
- **Axiom 3 — Self as folded boundary.** A self is the recursively stabilized boundary where world-resistance and other-resistance fold into internal constraints.
- **Axiom 4 — Consciousness as monitoring.** Consciousness is the monitoring of boundary displacement (Δϕ_boundary), enabling re-entry stabilization. Monitoring here is defined by write-back: the boundary-displacement signal must re-enter the next transition selection, not merely be detected.

## 3. Definitions

- **World.** Persistent incoming Δϕ flux that resists full prediction: a continuing source of mismatch.
- **Other.** A localized, irreducible Δϕ source that repeatedly forces boundary reconfiguration. Operational criterion: Other is the portion of externality whose mismatch remains persistent after available model updates; it is not absorbed by re-entry refinement within the system’s current H/G capacity.
- **Self.** A recursively stabilized boundary that maintains transition continuity by internalizing world- and other-induced constraints.
- **Consciousness.** Boundary-change monitoring: the process that flags mismatch and triggers self-model updates.
- **Meaning.** The residue of stabilized boundary re-entry: a boundary pattern that persists across transitions. Meaning persists when stabilized boundary re-entry keeps effective mismatch and update cost below the collapse threshold under irreversibility.

## 5. Structural propositions

- **P1.** World and other differ by scale, not by ontological kind: `Other ⊂ World`.
- **P2.** Self does not perceive the world; self is produced where world-resistance occurs and is folded into internal constraints.
- **P3.** Consciousness is not a substance but an interface: it marks boundary displacement to keep re-entry stable under irreversibility.

## 6. Consequences

- World, other, self, consciousness, and meaning are positions within the same boundary dynamics, not separate domains.
- A purely closed system tends to drift to saturation (`Δϕ → 0`) or divergence; sustained re-entry requires externality.
- This closes the ontological layer needed for later treatment of memory, affect, and governance constraints.

## 7. Exit question

If self is a folded boundary and consciousness monitors boundary displacement, why do some boundary events become persistent (meaning), while others dissolve? This motivates the next paper on memory as re-entry persistence.
