# Do-Not-Use Conditions and Common Misreadings

## Do not use SDeltaPhi-63 to claim all theories are merely tools

Theories can have history, depth, practice, community, and lived meaning. Module conversion extracts operational detection structure; it does not exhaust the theory.

## Do not use SDeltaPhi-63 to erase historical depth

Do not flatten religion, philosophy, ethics, or political theory into keywords.

## Do not use SDeltaPhi-63 to trivialize religion, philosophy, ethics, or politics

Conversion is not mockery or reduction.

## Do not use SDeltaPhi-63 as a final truth detector

It converts theories into conditional detectors. It does not decide ultimate truth.

## Do not use a converted module outside its scope

Every module requires activation conditions and trace.

## Do not treat module output as final reality

Module output is conditional and revisable.

## Do not use one theory-module to explain everything

This creates ideology capture.

## Do not remove UMR

A theory often leaves unresolved residue.

## Do not use conversion as intellectual colonization

Do not claim SDeltaPhi absorbs or owns the theory. It only converts selected operational features for AI use.

## Do not replace domain expertise with module conversion

Historical, theological, philosophical, legal, clinical, or technical expertise may still be necessary.
