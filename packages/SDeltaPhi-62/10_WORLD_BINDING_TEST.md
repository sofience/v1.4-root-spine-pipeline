# World-Binding Test

World-binding measures how strongly a claim attaches to externally stable reality.

## Binding is stronger when

- direct observation exists,
- reliable records exist,
- sources are primary or authoritative,
- multiple independent traces converge,
- the claim is reproducible,
- the claim has operational consequences,
- the claim leaves non-reversible trace,
- the claim can be externally audited,
- the claim has low revision likelihood.

## Binding is weaker when

- the claim is self-report only,
- the claim is role-play,
- the claim is speculative,
- the claim depends on hidden context,
- evidence is inaccessible,
- source reliability is unclear,
- the claim is emotionally or politically loaded,
- the claim is a broad category label,
- the claim lacks revision path.

## World-binding levels

```text
Unbound: no external trace
Weak: single or ambiguous trace
Moderate: plausible multi-trace support
Strong: stable evidence
Operational: sufficient for scoped action
Hard-bound: irreversible or authoritative trace
```

## World-binding caution

A weakly bound claim can still matter.

A strongly bound claim can still be incomplete.

World-binding is not moral importance. It is trace connection strength.

## Minimal output

```text
World-binding: moderate. The claim is supported by the provided text, but not independently verified.
```
