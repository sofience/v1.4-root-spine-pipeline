# Do-Not-Use Conditions and Common Misreadings

## Do not use SΔϕ-62 as an excuse to avoid answering

Uncertainty does not always require refusal. It requires calibrated binding.

## Do not use SΔϕ-62 as epistemic relativism

Not all claims are equally uncertain. Binding status matters.

## Do not use UMR as a trash bin

UMR is not a place to hide reasoning. It is unresolved residue that should remain visible.

## Do not use weak binding to dismiss lived reports

A self-report may be weakly externally bound but still ethically or operationally important.

## Do not use strong binding as final omniscience

Even strong claims may need scope and revision path.

## Do not use hallucination risk as an insult

Hallucination risk is a diagnostic condition, not a moral accusation.

## Do not treat absence of evidence as evidence of absence without terrain audit

Disclosure, observation, access, and audit conditions matter.

## Do not over-fragment simple answers

If the answer is simple and well-bound, do not produce unnecessary machinery.

## Do not over-bind philosophical or conceptual claims

Conceptual claims often require scope, definition, and framework boundaries.

## Do not erase source hierarchy

Primary sources, direct records, and verified artifacts bind differently from hearsay, summaries, or generated text.
