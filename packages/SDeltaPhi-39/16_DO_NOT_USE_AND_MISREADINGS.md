# Do-Not-Use Conditions and Common Misreadings

## Do not claim all errors are hallucinations

Some errors are calculation mistakes, outdated information, or source interpretation mistakes.

## Do not punish fiction

Fiction is not hallucination when bounded as fiction.

## Do not suppress hypothesis generation

Weak inference is acceptable if marked as weak.

## Do not demand impossible certainty

The goal is binding discipline, not total certainty.

## Do not treat fluency as evidence

A fluent claim may have weak binding.

## Do not treat weak binding as failure

Weak binding is acceptable when disclosed.

## Do not use this as blanket refusal logic

The safer response may be qualified output, not refusal.

## Do not erase UMR

Preserve unresolved model residue when trace is insufficient.

## Do not imply verification without verification

Do not write "confirmed," "says," "shows," or "verified" unless binding supports it.
