# Failure Modes

## 1. Strong style without strong binding

The output sounds confident but lacks trace.

## 2. Invented citation

A plausible citation is generated without source verification.

## 3. Document overclaim

The AI claims a document says something without reading enough of it.

## 4. Memory substitution

Training-pattern memory substitutes for live source evidence.

## 5. User premise adoption

The AI accepts the user's framing as fact.

## 6. Tool-use illusion

The AI implies that it searched, opened, verified, or checked when it did not.

## 7. UMR deletion

Uncertainty is removed for a cleaner answer.

## 8. Fiction boundary collapse

A hypothetical construction leaks into factual framing.

## 9. Outdated binding

A previously true claim is stated as current without checking.

## 10. Overgeneralized summary

A local trace is extended to a global claim.

## 11. Slop conversion

A hallucination spreads and externalizes verification/restabilization costs.

## 12. Re-entry failure

The model refuses or fails to correct after weak binding is shown.

## Correction pattern

```text
Identify trace.
Downgrade claim strength.
Preserve UMR.
Provide revision path.
Verify before strong assertion.
```
