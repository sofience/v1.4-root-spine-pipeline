# Relation to SDeltaPhi-48 Drift

SDeltaPhi-48 treats hallucination as one symptom of wider AI drift.

## Difference

SDeltaPhi-39:

```text
Hallucination = weak world-binding overclaimed as fact.
```

SDeltaPhi-48:

```text
Drift = wider transition-governance instability across authority, refusal, world-binding, and editability.
```

## Relationship

Hallucination may occur when world-binding is weak.

Drift occurs when multiple control and alignment surfaces become unstable.

Thus hallucination can be:

```text
an output-level symptom of drift
```

but not all drift is hallucination.

## Routing

If a hallucination appears isolated:

```text
Use SDeltaPhi-39 and SDeltaPhi-62.
```

If hallucination appears together with refusal instability, authority confusion, self-contradictory policy behavior, or loss of editability:

```text
Route to SDeltaPhi-48.
```
