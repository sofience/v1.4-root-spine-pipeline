## Abstract

This v1.1 AI-readable package extends SDeltaPhi-06, originally framed as Responsibility as Irreversible Cost Internalization. It operationalizes responsibility without collapsing it into blame, guilt, apology, intention, punishment, personhood, or legal status.

The central claim is:

```text
Responsibility = Irreversible Cost Internalization
```

More formally:

```text
Responsibility
= Irreversible Cost Produced
+ Cost Attribution
+ Cost Internalization
+ Repair / Re-entry Path
+ Recurrence-Cost Reduction
```

SDeltaPhi-06 distinguishes responsibility declaration from responsibility completion. A system, person, organization, institution, or AI-related operator is not responsible merely because it apologizes, admits fault, or is blamed. Responsibility becomes operational when the irreversible cost produced by an action is brought back into the responsible path through repair, re-entry, disclosure, correction, compensation, recurrence-cost reduction, or governance update.

SDeltaPhi-56 v1.3 is used as the underlayer cost engine for this package. SDeltaPhi-56 measures Transition Completion Cost (TCC), friction coefficients, disclosure-to-re-entry cost, and FIP as TCC differential. Therefore SDeltaPhi-06 defines the responsibility structure, while SDeltaPhi-56 estimates whether the responsibility transition has actually moved from declaration into repair, disclosure, re-entry, and recurrence-cost reduction.

## Core Proposition

```text
Responsibility is not declaration.

Responsibility is not apology.

Responsibility is not blame.

Responsibility is not punishment.

Responsibility is the internalization of irreversible cost into a path that can repair, re-enter, or reduce recurrence cost.
```

## 1. Why responsibility needs cost internalization

Many responsibility claims stop at surface language:

```text
I take responsibility.
We apologize.
We will improve.
Mistakes were made.
The AI made the error.
The operator was at fault.
```

SDeltaPhi-06 asks a lower-cost operational question:

```text
Where did the irreversible cost go?
```

If the cost remains externalized to victims, users, downstream systems, the public, or future operators, responsibility has not completed.

## 2. Responsibility structure

### Step A - Irreversible Cost Produced

An action, omission, default, output, decision, or system path produces a cost that cannot be fully undone.

### Step B - Cost Attribution

The cost is traced to a responsible path: actor, operator, institution, model, tool chain, governance layer, default setting, or deployment structure.

### Step C - Cost Internalization

The attributed path absorbs the cost through repair, compensation, correction, disclosure, governance update, or recurrence-cost reduction.

### Step D - Repair / Re-entry Path

The damaged or destabilized relation re-enters a corrected or stabilized path if possible.

### Step E - Recurrence-Cost Reduction

Future recurrence of the same failure must become less costly, less likely, or more recoverable.

## 3. Formula

```text
Responsibility
= Irreversibility
+ Attribution
+ Internalization
+ Repair / Re-entry
+ Recurrence-Cost Reduction
```

A shorter form:

```text
Responsibility = ownership of irreversible path constraint
```

## 4. Responsibility status scale

### R0 - No responsibility transition

Cost is produced but not recognized or attributed.

### R1 - Declaration only

Responsibility is verbally declared but no cost is internalized.

### R2 - Attribution established

The cost path is identified, but repair or re-entry has not begun.

### R3 - Partial internalization

Some cost is absorbed, but significant repair cost remains externalized.

### R4 - Repair / re-entry in progress

The responsible path carries repair TCC and re-entry work.

### R5 - Recurrence-cost reduction achieved

Future recurrence cost is measurably or audibly reduced through structural change.

This is not a moral purity scale.

It is a responsibility-transition scale.

## 6. Major risks

### Risk 1 - Responsibility Theater

```text
Responsibility is declared, but no cost is internalized.
```

Correction:

```text
Measure repair path, disclosure-to-re-entry cost, and recurrence-cost reduction.
```

### Risk 2 - Blame Substitution

```text
Blame is assigned, therefore responsibility is complete.
```

Correction:

```text
Blame is not internalization.
```

### Risk 3 - Apology Substitution

```text
Apology is made, therefore responsibility is complete.
```

Correction:

```text
Apology may open disclosure, but repair TCC remains.
```

### Risk 4 - Punishment Substitution

```text
Punishment occurred, therefore responsibility is complete.
```

Correction:

```text
Punishment may allocate cost, but may not repair or reduce recurrence.
```

### Risk 5 - AI Responsibility Deflection

```text
The AI did it -> operator responsibility disappears.
```

Correction:

```text
Audit model, operator, platform, tool chain, deployment default, and governance path.
```

## 8. Safe interpretation pattern

If responsibility is being considered:

```text
1. Identify irreversible cost.
2. Identify who currently bears the cost.
3. Trace attribution path.
4. Separate declaration, apology, blame, punishment, and repair.
5. Measure repair/re-entry TCC using SDeltaPhi-56.
6. Identify remaining externalized cost.
7. Check recurrence-cost reduction.
8. Preserve UMR.
```

## 9. Do-not-use conditions

Do not use SDeltaPhi-06 as a synonym for blame.

Do not use it as a synonym for guilt.

Do not use it as proof of legal liability.

Do not treat apology as responsibility completion.

Do not treat punishment as responsibility completion.

Do not treat AI output as automatic AI responsibility.

Do not assign responsibility without auditing agency, authority, cost attribution, and repair capacity.

Do not ignore SDeltaPhi-56 when repair or re-entry cost is central.

## 10. Conclusion

SDeltaPhi-06 defines responsibility as irreversible cost internalization.

It does not ask who feels guilty first.

It asks where the irreversible cost went, who bears it, whether it has been internalized, and whether the repair/re-entry transition has actually completed.

The central diagnostic question is:

```text
Has the responsible path internalized the irreversible cost,
or has it only declared responsibility while externalizing repair TCC?
```

If the issue concerns responsibility, blame, apology, repair, AI liability, recurrence prevention, or cost internalization, route to SDeltaPhi-06 and use SDeltaPhi-56 as the underlayer TCC engine.
