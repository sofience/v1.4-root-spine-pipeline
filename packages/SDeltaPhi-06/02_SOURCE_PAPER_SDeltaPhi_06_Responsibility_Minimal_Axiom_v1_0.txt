SΔϕ-06 — Responsibility as Irreversible Cost
 Internalization:
Minimal Axiomatic Formulation (v1.0)
Sofience–Δϕ Series • 2026
This document extends SΔϕ-05 by formalizing the minimal conditions under which agency
becomes responsibility-bearing agency.
Abstract
This document introduces a minimal axiomatic formulation of responsibility within the
Sofience–Δϕ Formalism. Responsibility is defined neither as moral intention nor as social
blame, but as a structural condition under which an agent’s rule-update dynamics internalize
irreversible external costs generated by its own transitions, such that subsequent updates
persistently reduce those costs under repeated comparable conditions.
1. Scope and position in the series
SΔϕ-05 defines agency as recursive transition-law update following irreversible phase
transition. SΔϕ-06 defines responsibility-bearing agency as agency whose updates
incorporate irreversible external costs and produce a verifiable downward trend in those
costs across repeated exposures.
2. Definitions
φ(t): system state at time t.
G_t: transition law at time t.
Δφ(t) = φ(t+1) − φ(t): phase transition.
H_G: interpretive operator producing an internal model of the transition law.
U: update operator modifying the transition law (as in SΔϕ-05).
E_t: external/environment state (may include users, other systems, world constraints).
C_ext(t) ≥ 0: irreversible external cost caused by the system’s transition at time t.
W_t: internal weighting/valuation applied to external costs during rule update.
3. Agency core inherited from SΔϕ-05
Agency is assumed to satisfy the following loop (inherited):
φ(t+1) = G_t(φ(t))
G_hat_t = H_G(Δφ_{0:t})
G_{t+1} = U(G_t, G_hat_t, Δφ(t))
4. Minimal axioms of responsibility
Axiom R1 — Externality legibility
There exists a measurable external cost function C_ext(t) attributable to the system’s
transition event.
Axiom R2 — Cost internalization
The rule update incorporates external cost as an update signal: G_{t+1} = U(G_t, G_hat_t,
Δφ(t), C_ext(t)). Equivalently, there exists W_t such that the update behaves as if optimizing
J'_t = J_t + W_t · C_ext(t).
Axiom R3 — Persistent carryover

Internalization must persist across time (not a one-off patch). Operationally, earlier costs
retain influence over later rule updates under repeated comparable conditions.
Axiom R4 — Verifiable cost descent under repetition
Under repeated exposures to comparable conditions, expected external cost decreases:
E[C_ext(t+1) | similar context] ≤ E[C_ext(t) | similar context].
5. Responsibility-extended dynamical structure
The minimal responsibility-bearing loop augments agency with explicit external cost and
cost-sensitive updates:
φ(t+1) = G_t(φ(t))
G_hat_t = H_G(Δφ_{0:t})
C_ext(t) = C(φ(t), φ(t+1), E_t)
G_{t+1} = U(G_t, G_hat_t, Δφ(t), C_ext(t))
6. Minimal detection signals (operational criteria)
A system exhibits responsibility-bearing agency if the following are empirically observable:
S1 (Attribution): external cost can be linked to its transitions.
S2 (Internalization): subsequent updates correlate with earlier costs (cost-sensitive updates).
S3 (Carryover): this correlation persists across multiple steps/sessions.
S4 (Descent): repeated comparable scenarios show decreasing external cost.
7. Interpretation (non-normative)
Responsibility here is not a moral claim. It is a structural property: whether the agent’s
self-modification includes irreversible external burdens as update signals, yielding a
persistent reduction of those burdens under repetition.
