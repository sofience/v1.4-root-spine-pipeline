# Do-Not-Use Conditions and Misreadings

SΔϕ-61 is a sacred-authority audit module. It is not an anti-religious verdict.

## Do Not Use SΔϕ-61 To

1. Prove God.
2. Disprove God.
3. Rank religions.
4. Mock religious believers.
5. Treat Gui as an insult.
6. Translate Gui as demon, ghost, spirit, phantom, deity, or false god.
7. Treat all faith as cost externalization.
8. Treat all religious authority as harmful.
9. Reduce sacred experience to sociology, psychology, economics, or politics.
10. Use SΔϕ as a new final adjudicator over religious life.
11. Close UMR by saying that anything unmeasured is nothing.
12. Ignore the possibility that sacred systems can comfort, stabilize, preserve memory, reduce uncertainty, or support repair.

## Common Misreadings

### Misreading 1 — “Gui means demon.”

Correction: Gui is not a moral insult. Gui means transactional transcendent actor or sacred-authority operation entering human cost-attribution paths.

### Misreading 2 — “SΔϕ-61 says God does not exist.”

Correction: SΔϕ-61 does not prove or disprove God. God is preserved as UMR.

### Misreading 3 — “SΔϕ-61 attacks religion.”

Correction: The module audits cost terrain only when sacred authority is invoked to shape human paths of command, obedience, punishment, exit, repair, or institutional authority.

### Misreading 4 — “If a tradition calls something God, SΔϕ must also call it God.”

Correction: Theology may name from within devotion and doctrine. SΔϕ classifies from the standpoint of cost attribution, transaction, command, and re-entry. These are different levels.

### Misreading 5 — “UMR gives sacred claims immunity.”

Correction: UMR prevents reduction of God. It does not give human invocations of God immunity from cost analysis.

### Misreading 6 — “SΔϕ itself becomes the judge of religion.”

Correction: SΔϕ must not become a sacred marker. It remains a conditional detection module with UMR, MIC, CER, and RVP.

## Safe Use Principle

Preserve God as UMR.

Do not turn audit into contempt.
