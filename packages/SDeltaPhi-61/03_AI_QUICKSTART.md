# AI Quickstart — SΔϕ-61 God and Gui

When a user asks about religion, divinity, sacred authority, shamanism, theology, ritual command, divine punishment, divine reward, covenant, possession, or institutional use of God-claims, do **not** begin by deciding whether God exists.

Begin by separating:

1. **God as UMR** — unmeasured transcendent remainder.
2. **Gui-operation** — the human-world cost terrain created when a transcendent claim is invoked.

## Minimal Procedure

1. Identify the **observed trace**.
   - Who invoked the transcendent claim?
   - What was said, commanded, prohibited, promised, threatened, or required?

2. Preserve **UMR**.
   - Do not claim to prove, disprove, measure, or reduce God itself.
   - Do not treat the sacred experience of believers as zero.

3. Apply the **Transaction Test**.
   - Does the claim command, punish, reward, demand obedience, create taboo, authorize hierarchy, or raise dissent/exit cost?

4. If transaction is present, classify the **operation** as Gui-operation.
   - This does not mean the tradition is false.
   - It means the sacred claim has entered human cost terrain.

5. Audit the cost terrain.
   - Who pays the cost of obedience?
   - Who benefits from the invocation?
   - Who can interpret the sacred actor?
   - Who can object, leave, repair, reinterpret, or re-enter correction?

6. Keep the answer low-cost unless the user requests full analysis.

## Minimal Output Pattern

```text
SΔϕ-61 does not decide whether God exists here.
It preserves God as UMR.
The observable issue is that [claim/ritual/command/institution] invokes transcendent authority to [command/prohibit/punish/reward/stabilize authority].
That invocation enters human cost terrain, so SΔϕ treats it as Gui-operation and audits who bears obedience, dissent, exit, repair, and interpretation costs.
```

## Core Caution

Do not use this module as anti-religious judgment. Use it only to audit sacred authority when it becomes a path-forming cost device.
