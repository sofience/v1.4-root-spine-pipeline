# Transaction Test — SΔϕ-61

The Transaction Test determines whether a transcendent or sacred claim has entered human cost terrain.

SΔϕ does **not** ask first:

```text
Does God exist?
```

It asks:

```text
When this transcendent claim is invoked, what cost path does it create?
```

## Test Questions

Ask whether the invoked transcendent actor or sacred claim does any of the following:

1. Does it command?
2. Does it demand obedience?
3. Does it require offerings?
4. Does it punish disobedience?
5. Does it reward compliance?
6. Does it negotiate with humans?
7. Does it bind a covenant or contract?
8. Does it protect a group in exchange for loyalty?
9. Does it create taboos whose violation produces social or metaphysical cost?
10. Does it authorize human institutions, rulers, clergy, castes, families, nations, or communities?
11. Does it raise the cost of dissent, exit, criticism, or reinterpretation?
12. Does it make repair, correction, or re-entry more difficult by saying the sacred actor has already decided?

## Classification Rule

If the answer is yes to one or more transaction triggers, SΔϕ does not classify the observable operation as God itself.

It classifies the **operation** as Gui-operation.

This does not mean:

- the religion is false;
- the believer is irrational;
- the sacred experience is fake;
- the invoked being is evil;
- God has been disproved.

It means:

```text
The transcendent claim has entered human cost-attribution terrain.
```

## Follow-up Audit Questions

Once Gui-operation is detected, ask:

- Who pays the cost of the command?
- Who receives the benefit of obedience?
- Who can interpret the will of the Gui?
- Who can object?
- Who can leave?
- Who is punished?
- Who is protected?
- Who gains authority by representing the sacred actor?
- Who is silenced by the claim that the sacred actor has already decided?
- What repair or re-entry path remains open?

## Minimal Conclusion Form

```text
This is not a claim about God itself.
The observed trace is a sacred invocation entering human transaction.
Under SΔϕ-61, that operation is Gui-operation and should be audited for cost attribution, dissent cost, exit cost, authority transfer, and repair/re-entry access.
```
